# Unschooling in the United Arab Emirates

## Compulsory education

Under *Federal Decree-Law No. 39 of 2022 Concerning Compulsory Education*,
which entered into force on 2 January 2023:

* education is compulsory for UAE citizens from the primary stage until the
  end of school education; and
* education is compulsory for resident non-citizens until the end of the
  primary stage.

The obligation falls on the child’s caregiver, who must enrol the child when
the appropriate age is reached and ensure continuity and regular attendance.

The law defines compulsory education by educational stage rather than by a
single fixed age range. For the 2026–2027 academic year, children entering
Grade 1 in schools beginning in August or September must generally have reached
the age of six by 31 December of the admission year.

For resident non-citizens, the federal compulsory-education obligation ends
with the primary stage. This does not, however, create a generally recognised
or regulated right to family-directed unschooling during the compulsory stage.

## Home study

The Ministry of Education operates a formal **Home-Study Track** within its
Integrated Continuing Education system.

Despite sometimes being described in English as “homeschooling”, this is not
an elective home-education route for ordinarily school-aged children. It is a
self-study programme for older students who have exceeded the maximum age for
admission to regular public or private education.

The programme:

* is available to UAE citizens and residents;
* covers Grades 7–12;
* uses the Ministry of Education’s general-track curriculum;
* requires students to study independently rather than attend regular classes;
  and
* requires students to sit Ministry examinations.

The minimum ages stated in the Ministry’s 2025–2026 registration guide are:

| Grade | Minimum age |
| ----- | ----------: |
| 7     |          14 |
| 8     |          15 |
| 9     |          16 |
| 10    |          17 |
| 11    |          18 |
| 12    |          19 |

Applicants normally require evidence of the last school year they successfully
completed. Certificates issued abroad or under a non-Ministry curriculum may
require attestation and equivalency.

Applicants without acceptable previous school records may, in limited
circumstances, enter through a level-assessment procedure for Grades 7–9.

Registration takes place during a prescribed annual period and applications
must be approved by the Ministry. The programme therefore cannot ordinarily be
started immediately at any point during the year.

Because the curriculum, progression requirements and examinations are
prescribed by the Ministry, the Home-Study Track cannot operate as
interest-driven unschooling.

Successful completion is treated as equivalent to completion through the
regular public-school system.

## Dubai: Rahhal

Dubai’s Knowledge and Human Development Authority operates **Rahhal**, a
framework through which a Dubai private school may arrange a more flexible or
individualised programme for a student.

Rahhal is not independent home education.

The child must remain registered as a full-time student at a KHDA-regulated
Dubai private school. Participation by schools is voluntary, the school must
agree to the arrangement, and the individual programme requires KHDA approval.

Learning may take place partly at home, online or through alternative
activities, but the student remains enrolled in the school and must continue
to meet applicable curriculum requirements, including compulsory UAE subjects.

Rahhal may accommodate a child’s talents, interests or individual
circumstances, but it remains a school-designed and officially approved
programme rather than autonomous, family-directed unschooling.

Outside Dubai, no equivalent general homeschooling framework was identified.
The federal Ministry of Education’s rules therefore remain the principal
formal route, subject to any arrangements made through the relevant local
education authority.

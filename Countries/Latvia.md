# Unschooling in Latvia

## Compulsory education

In Latvia, completion of a preschool education programme is compulsory from
the age of five. Basic education comprises nine grades, normally beginning in
the calendar year in which the child turns seven, and must be completed or
continued until the child reaches the age of 18.

## Unschooling

Education in the family (« izglītība ģimenē ») is permitted at the preschool
level and during the first stage of basic education, comprising grades 1–6.

At preschool level, a preschool education programme may be implemented in the
family with methodological assistance from an educational institution
implementing a licensed preschool programme or from a preschool education
consultative centre.

For grades 1–6, except under a special basic education programme, the child
must first be enrolled in a school. Under Cabinet Regulation No. 11 of
11 January 2022, paragraph 12, the head of the school may, on the basis of a
parental application, authorise the child to acquire the relevant
basic-education content in the family for one school year, provided that the
application is accompanied by either a certificate issued by the family
doctor or treating doctor concerning a health condition, or an opinion issued
by a clinical and health psychologist concerning a psychological condition,
because of which the child needs to acquire that content in the family. The
school must give the parents continuing access to the applicable curriculum
and programme materials, provide an opportunity for consultation with a
subject teacher at least once a month, and assess the child’s attainment at
least twice per semester.

Education in the family is not available during the second stage of basic
education, comprising grades 7–9. Pupils studying outside ordinary school
attendance have instead generally used part-time attendance (« nepilna laika
klātiene », formerly « neklātiene ») or distance-learning (« tālmācība »)
programmes.

According to the General Education Law :

> Article 20.¹ Mandatory preparation of preschool children
>
> (1) Completion of the preschool education programme is mandatory for
> children from the age of five.

## News

Since 1 September 2025, the phased statutory transition to instruction in
Latvian throughout basic education has been completed in state, municipal and
private schools.

On 19 February 2026, following reconsideration requested by State President
Edgars Rinkēvičs, the Saeima readopted amendments to the Education Law
restricting distance learning and education in the family during grades 1–6.
The amendments entered into force on 19 March 2026, but the application of
the new restrictions was postponed ; the current regime therefore continues
to apply until the dates below.

For education in the family, the new regime will apply to all grades 1–6 from
1 September 2027. A pupil will then be permitted to study in the family for
one school year where the municipality has assessed that, because of special
social, health, educational-access or other substantial circumstances, this
form of education is consistent with the child’s best interests. The present
medical or psychological authorisation by the head of the school will
therefore be replaced by a mandatory municipal best-interests assessment,
moving the decision on the admissibility of this form of education from the
head of the school to the municipality. The municipality will be able to
extend the period covered by its assessment. The Cabinet of Ministers is
responsible for establishing the assessment criteria and procedure.

The restrictions on distance learning will be introduced progressively :
grades 1 and 4 from 1 September 2027 ; grades 2 and 5 from 1 September 2028 ;
grades 3 and 6 from 1 September 2029. Separate exceptions will continue to
permit distance learning, but not education in the family, for pupils
permanently resident abroad who have no other accessible form of Latvian
education and for persons aged 18 or over who have not completed the first
stage of basic education.

In June 2026, the Saeima rejected an opposition proposal to repeal the new
restrictions.

According to Ministry of Education and Science data for 2024, approximately
3 500 pupils were enrolled in distance learning at the basic-education level,
including 1 100 pupils in grades 1–6. Across all educational levels,
approximately 12 800 pupils were enrolled in distance learning.

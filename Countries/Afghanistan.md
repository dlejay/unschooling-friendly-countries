# Unschooling in Afghanistan

## Compulsory education

Under the Education Law of 2008, schooling is compulsory for 9 years.
The de facto authorities rule by decree in the absence of a constitution, and laws inherited from the republican period are applied only selectively, so the current enforceability of this requirement is uncertain.
Since September 2021, girls are barred from education beyond grade 6, so in practice girls receive at most 6 years of schooling.

## Unschooling

Afghanistan is governed by decree in the absence of a constitution.
According to a report from Rahmatullah Amiri and Ashley Jackson :
> Ensuring all forms of education respect and adhere to Islam is a central principle.

## News

UNICEF and partners run Community-Based Education (CBE) classes that function like local schools. These classes are held in community buildings, houses and sometimes mosques, with the agreement of the de facto authorities ; teachers are recruited using Ministry of Education testing criteria. In 2025, 442,000 children (66% girls) benefited from community-based learning.
Separately, underground schools and online programmes serve as a lifeline for girls barred from secondary education ; in some areas local authorities informally tolerate them. Both were undermined in 2025 by cuts to international funding.

# Unschooling in Honduras

## Compulsory education

The _Ley Fundamental de Educación_ (Decreto 262-2011) makes education free and compulsory
from pre-primary through the end of secondary. Article 10 places the obligation on parents
and guardians, who must ensure their children complete at least one year of _Educación
Prebásica_, all of _Educación Básica_ and all of _Educación Media_.

In practice :

- _Prebásica_ : reference ages 4–6 ; the third year (ages 5–6) is the compulsory one. The
  pre-primary prerequisite for entering básica applies “ _donde existen condiciones de
  cobertura_ ” — only where coverage exists ;
- _Básica_ : nine compulsory years, reference ages 6–14, in three sequential cycles of
  three years ;
- _Media_ : reference ages 15–17 — two years for the _Bachillerato Científico Humanista_,
  three for the _Bachillerato Técnico Profesional_.

## Home education

Home education (_Educación en Casa_) is a recognised _modalidad_ of the national education
system, listed in Article 27 of the _Ley Fundamental de Educación_, whose definitions
provide that it may be offered in two forms : (a) _educación libre o no escolarizada_, and
(b) _educación curricular_, under the supervision of an educational establishment of the
corresponding level. Articles 5–6 of the _Reglamento de Educación en Casa_ (Acuerdo
1367-SE-2014) restate this distinction, the free form allowing the parent, tutor or adult
learner to decide the type of learning desired according to the learner’s own interests,
subject to accreditation. The 2014 Regulation contemplated direct registration with the
Departmental Education Directorate, confirmed within fifteen days.

The operational framework — _Lineamientos para la Funcionalidad de la Modalidad de
Educación en Casa_ (Acuerdo 0368-SE-2020, issued 2 October 2020, published in La Gaceta
on 3 June 2021) — has however made the regime school-anchored for all school-age
learners, without distinguishing between the two forms :

- access grounds include the parents’ simple choice of the modality, provided they meet
  the required academic and pedagogical conditions (Art. 9) — the modality is open to all ;
- parents’ express obligations include educating through tutors “ _con el apoyo y
  seguimiento de una institución educativa, debidamente autorizada_ ” (Art. 44) and
  “ _sujetarse estrictamente al Currículo oficial_ ”, the academic calendar, and the
  prescribed supervision and evaluation processes (Art. 45) ;
- every learner is enrolled for a ten-month grade cycle, with results recorded in SACE ;
  enrolment runs through a chosen educational centre with authorisation from the municipal
  or district education authority, including a home-observation visit (Arts. 2–3, 13–20) ;
- learners are supervised by the educational centre to which they are attached (Art. 48) ;
  assessment uses the official _malla curricular_, official textbooks and workbooks,
  monthly and end-of-grade tests and term evaluations sat preferably at the accrediting
  centre, plus a curriculum-aligned portfolio, community service (TES) and a National
  Anthem examination (Arts. 42, 52–61) ;
- the tutor need not be a certified teacher, but must be over 18 and meet
  educational-background requirements — completed secondary for prebásica and the first
  two cycles of básica, university studies for the third cycle and media (Arts. 37–38) ;
- a foreign curriculum must be approved (“ _avalado_ ”) by the Secretaría de Educación
  (Art. 51) ;
- accreditation applies to the modality “ _en cualquiera de sus formas de entrega_ ”
  (Art. 55) ;
- decisively, Article 10 of the Lineamientos reinterprets “ _educación libre o no
  escolarizada_ ” as continuing education through short vocational courses — a non-formal,
  adult-oriented reading that leaves no identifiable route for a compulsory-school-age
  child to substitute interest-led content for the prescribed curriculum.

There is thus a genuine tension between the interest-based wording of the 2014 Regulation
and the later operational rules, but no currently practicable path to interest-driven
learning : registration, grade-level curriculum, institutional supervision and formal
assessment remain compulsory. Any remaining flexibility concerns principally the place,
pace and daily organisation of learning. (A separate prebásica-only programme named
EDUCAS serves communities lacking a pre-primary centre.)

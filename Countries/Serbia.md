# Unschooling in Serbia

## Compulsory education

Serbian law mandates eight years of primary education for all children. Primary schooling is compulsory and normally covers eight grades.

## Unschooling

Although Serbian law gives parents the right in principle to arrange primary education at home, this is not an open-ended homeschooling regime in practice.

> Article 38
>
> A parent or other legal representative has the right to enable his child to receive basic education and upbringing at home, while providing for the costs of education and upbringing.

The statute grants a general right in principle, but the 2020 ministerial bylaw narrows access in practice. Under Article 6, home education is available only to a pupil, other than a first-grade or eighth/final-grade pupil, who for justified reasons (*iz opravdanih razloga*) cannot attend school in person; the bylaw gives examples including active athletes, pupils in music or ballet school, poor local infrastructure, and temporary stays abroad due to a parent’s work. Articles 7 and 8 then require the parent to notify the school and submit a request with supporting documentation, after which the school director obtains the opinion of an expert commission and issues a written approval decision.

Home-schoolers must achieve the prescribed goals, outcomes, and achievement standards of the official curriculum. Adapted goals and outcomes are permitted for children with disabilities educated under IOP-2.

A 2020 ministerial bylaw — the *Pravilnik o bližim uslovima za ostvarivanje i način osiguranja kvaliteta i vrednovanja nastave kod kuće za učenike osnovne škole* (*Službeni glasnik RS* 109/2020) — provides that a home-schooled pupil may advance at most two grades per primary-education cycle by exam. To sit an exam, the pupil must have covered at least two-thirds of the annual instruction for the subject. If a student fails an exam, they may retake it once; failing the retake means repeating the grade and continuing education in school.

A parallel statutory regime exists at secondary level under Article 27 of the Law on Secondary Education.

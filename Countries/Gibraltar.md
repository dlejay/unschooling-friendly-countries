# Unschooling in Gibraltar

Gibraltar is a British Overseas Territory with full legislative autonomy on education. UK statutes (Education Act 1996, Children’s Wellbeing and Schools Act 2026, the forthcoming CNIS register, etc.) do not extend to Gibraltar. Education is governed by Gibraltar’s own Education and Training Act (Act No. 1974-11), as amended.

## Compulsory education

Under section 49(1) of the Education and Training Act, compulsory school age runs from age 4 to age 16. By virtue of section 2(4), a child whose 16th birthday falls during a school year is deemed to attain that age on the last day of that school year, so in practice the duty extends to the end of the school year in which the child turns 16. Section 49(2) gives the Minister an order-making power to extend the upper limit to 18, but no such order has been made.

Section 18 organises the system in three stages : primary, secondary, further. Section 19 frames primary as ages 4–11 and secondary as ages 11–16 (with optional continuation to 19).

## Home education

The duty falls on the parent under section 50 of the Education and Training Act, as amended by Act 2023-19 :

> 50. Subject to the provisions of section 73 it shall be the duty of the parent of every child of compulsory school age residing in Gibraltar, to cause them to receive suitable education which, in the opinion of the Director is appropriate–
>
> (a) to their age, ability and aptitude ; and
>
> (b) to any SEND they may have,
>
> either by regular attendance at school or otherwise.

The “or otherwise” limb permits home education. There is no statutory curriculum, no statutory pre-approval requirement, and no home-education registration scheme in the Act itself. Suitability is assessed by the Director against the section 50 standard, which now imports a SEND limb mirroring the structure of the English Education Act 1996 s.7. Section 51 sets out the school attendance order procedure that follows if the Director is not satisfied that the child is receiving suitable education.

Citizens Advice Bureau Gibraltar advises parents who intend to educate at home to inform the Director of Education and Training of their intention and to be ready to demonstrate that the education is suitable. The CAB page is useful as practical guidance but its statement that compulsory school age runs “from the age of 4 to the age of 15” is stale post-2023 ; the current upper bound is 16.

## News

**Education and Training (Amendment) Act 2023 (No. 2023-19), commenced 27 July 2023.** Wide-ranging modernisation of the principal Act. Changes most relevant to compulsory and home education :

- Section 49 was rewritten to fix compulsory school age in the primary text as 4 to 16. Pre-2023 the primary text was 5 to 15, with a proviso allowing the Minister by order to lower the floor to 4 and raise the ceiling to 16 ; the 2023 Act folded those bounds into the substantive provision and replaced the old proviso with a new one allowing further extension to 18.
- Section 19 was rewritten so that primary is ages 4–11 and secondary 11–16 (with optional 16–19). Pre-2023 the stages were primary 5–12 and secondary 12–15.
- Section 50 was rewritten to use “suitable education” (rather than “efficient full-time education”) and to add a SEND limb alongside the traditional “age, ability and aptitude” criterion. The “or otherwise” limb permitting home education is preserved.
- Other parts of the Act were rewritten more substantially (SEND framework, behaviour and discipline, suspension and reintegration, religious-education excusal, environmental-education duty), but these do not bear directly on home education.

**Education and Training (Amendment) Act 2024 (No. 2024-19), commenced 23 December 2024.** Touches ss. 2(1), 63(1)-(2) and 66 ; not relevant to compulsory schooling or home education.

**Communications (Amendment) Act 2025 (No. 2025-07), dated 11 July 2025.** Listed as an amending enactment of the Education and Training Act on the Laws of Gibraltar page, although not yet incorporated into the consolidated PDF (which is itself dated 23 December 2024). Separate commencement notices were issued in July, August, and October 2025 for various provisions. Connected to the Government’s policy of restricting personal mobile-phone use in schools ; Yondr pouches were rolled out at Bayside and Westside comprehensive schools in mid-January 2026 and were operational by March 2026. Does not bear on the home-education position.

# Unschooling in Austria

## Compulsory education

Austria operates an _Unterrichtspflicht_ (compulsory instruction) rather than a strict compulsory-school-attendance model, so general compulsory education may be fulfilled outside a public school. It lasts nine years and begins on the 1 September following the child’s sixth birthday.

## Unschooling

According to the _Schulpflichtgesetz_ :

> § 11. (1) Notwithstanding § 12, general compulsory education can also be fulfilled by attending classes at a private school without public status (_Öffentlichkeitsrecht_),
> provided that the classes are at least equivalent to those at a school named in § 5.
>
> (2) Compulsory schooling can also be fulfilled by attending home tuition (_häuslicher Unterricht_),
> provided that the tuition is at least equivalent to that at a school named in § 5 — with the exception of the polytechnic school (_Polytechnische Schule_).
>
> (2a) Paragraphs 1 and 2 do not apply to pupils who are required to attend a German support class (_Deutschförderklasse_) pursuant to § 8h para. 2 or a German support course (_Deutschförderkurs_) pursuant to § 8h para. 3 of the School Organisation Act (_Schulorganisationsgesetz_). These pupils must fulfil their general compulsory education requirement for as long as they need one of these special language support measures in public schools or in schools with _Öffentlichkeitsrecht_ that have a legally regulated school designation.
>
> (3) The parents or other legal guardians must notify the _Bildungsdirektion_ within one week after the end of the previous school year that their child is taking part in instruction mentioned in paragraph 1 or 2. The notification must include :
>
> - the first and last names, date of birth, and address of the person who will be primarily responsible for instructing the child ;
> - the location where the instruction will take place ;
> - the annual report card for the previous school year or a certificate of the external examination for the previous grade level ;
> - the curriculum to be followed and the grade level at which instruction will take place ; and
> - a summary of the pedagogical concept for the instruction.

The law applies to all Austrian residents, subject to the exclusion in § 11 Abs. 2a for pupils who are required to attend a German support class or course.

Participation must be notified afresh for each school year ; it is not a one-off entitlement. The _Bildungsdirektion_ must prohibit _häuslicher Unterricht_ and order school attendance where a statutory ground under § 11 Abs. 6 applies — among them that equivalence is unlikely to be attained, that the pupil is required to attend a German support class or course, that the _Reflexionsgespräch_ is not held, or that the annual _Externistenprüfung_ is not sat in time or does not demonstrate the required attainment.

Within _häuslicher Unterricht_, the skipping, changing, or repeating of grades is not possible, and instruction may not begin before the child reaches compulsory-school age. The parents or guardians are solely responsible for the child’s learning and development ; the school has no educational role and does not assist with the planning or delivery of instruction.

## Statistics

Registrations rose sharply during the COVID-19 pandemic, peaking in 2021/22, and then declined.

| Year    | Homeschoolers |
| ------- | ------------- |
| 2022/23 | 4,083         |
| 2021/22 | 7,515         |
| 2020/21 | 2,603         |
| 2019/20 | 2,444         |
| 2018/19 | 2,307         |

The figures are start-of-year notifications reported by the Bundesstelle für Sektenfragen. The 2022/23 figure had declined to 3,290 by the semester break, since a return to school is possible during the year. No verified primary-source figure for 2023/24 or later was available at the date of review.

## News

Two successive amendments tightened the regulation of _häuslicher Unterricht_ after registrations rose during the COVID-19 pandemic.

The first amendment (_Novelle_ 2022, BGBl. I Nr. 232/2021), applicable from the 2022/23 school year, brought the notification deadline forward to within one week of the end of the previous school year, introduced a mandatory _Reflexionsgespräch_ — a meeting on the pupil’s attainment, to be held no later than two weeks after the end of the semester break at the school the pupil would attend if _häuslicher Unterricht_ were prohibited — and centralised the _Externistenprüfungen_, which are taken before examination commissions established by the _Bildungsdirektion_.

The second amendment (_Novelle_ 2023, BGBl. I Nr. 37/2023, in force April 2023) added the requirement to submit a summary of the _pädagogisches Konzept_ with the notification and consolidated the statutory grounds on which the _Bildungsdirektion_ must prohibit participation (§ 11 Abs. 6).

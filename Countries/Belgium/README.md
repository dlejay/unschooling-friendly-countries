# Unschooling in Belgium

## Communities

Education is a Community competence under Art. 127 §1 of the *Constitution*. The three Communities legislate independently for their respective education systems, and the home-education regime accordingly differs across the country.

|       | Community                       |
| ----- | ------------------------------- |
| **✗** | [Flanders](Flanders.md)         |
| **✗** | [Fédération Wallonie-Bruxelles](Wallonia-Brussels.md)   |
| **✗** | [Deutschsprachige Gemeinschaft](German-speaking-Community.md)  |

The Flemish Community covers Dutch-medium education across the Flemish Region and in the bilingual Brussels-Capital Region. The Fédération Wallonie-Bruxelles covers French-medium education across the Walloon Region (except the German-speaking municipalities) and in Brussels. The Deutschsprachige Gemeinschaft covers the nine German-speaking municipalities of the eastern cantons.

## Compulsory education

Compulsory education runs from age 5 to 18 under the federal *Wet van 29 juni 1983 betreffende de leerplicht* — *loi du 29 juin 1983 concernant l'obligation scolaire*. The starting age was lowered from 6 to 5 by the *Wet van 23 maart 2019* (*loi du 23 mars 2019*) following the constitutional revision that transferred the power to set it to the federal legislator. The *Leerplichtwet* applies to every minor residing in Belgium, irrespective of nationality.

## Home education

The Constitution guarantees a free choice of education (Art. 24 §1). Across all three Communities, compulsory *education* does not mean compulsory *schooling* : parents may choose home education in lieu of school enrolment. In every Community, home education is conditional — on annual declaration, on substantive aims, and on periodic examination at the relevant central examination board. The specifics of declaration, inspection, and examination differ by Community and are covered in each Community file.

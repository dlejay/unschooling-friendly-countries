# Unschooling in the Fédération Wallonie-Bruxelles

## Legal framework

Home education (*enseignement à domicile*, EAD) in the French-speaking Community of Belgium is governed by the *Code de l'enseignement fondamental et de l'enseignement secondaire*, Art. 1.7.1-12 to 1.7.1-24. These provisions, codified by the *décret du 3 mai 2019 portant les livres 1er et 2 du Code* (M.B. 19-09-2019) and amended notably by the *décret du 24 février 2022*, integrated and replaced the operative EAD framework of the *décret du 25 avril 2008*, whose original Chapter III provisions were abrogated as of 1 September 2020; the 2008 decree now survives only in residual and transitional form. The regime is conditional on annual declaration, substantive aims, certification milestones, and inspection.

Art. 1.7.1-12 defines EAD as instruction given to a minor subject to compulsory education who is not enrolled in a school organised, subsidised, or recognised by any of the three Communities. Two regimes coexist: individual home education, in which parents teach the child or hire a private tutor; and private-school home education, in which the child attends a non-recognised establishment. A limited number of foreign, European, neighbouring-state, or international establishments are individually recognised as satisfying compulsory education and exempt parents from the EAD declaration.

## Access and declaration

Cumulative access conditions: the child must be of compulsory-education age (5 to 18), domiciled in the Walloon Region or the Brussels-Capital Region, and not enrolled in an establishment whose attendance satisfies compulsory education for FWB purposes. From age 12, the child must also hold the certification corresponding to their age under the certification sequence then in force (see *Pedagogical constraints and controls*), failing which access to EAD closes for the following year, subject to a reasoned derogation request decided by the *Commission de l'enseignement à domicile* after opinion of the *Service général de l'Inspection*. Derogation files typically require a *plan individuel de formation* (PIF) and documentation of the child's prior schooling.

The *déclaration d'enseignement à domicile* must be filed annually, per child, by **5 September**, via the *Mon Espace* portal or by registered post. The deadline extends to 15 September where the child was enrolled at the start of the school year in an establishment whose attendance satisfies compulsory education, and to 60 days from registration in the population or foreigners' register for families establishing residence in Belgium mid-year. Late, incomplete, or manifestly inadmissible declarations may be rejected, triggering compulsory enrolment. Failure to declare is sanctioned by a fine of €25 to €200, doubled on repeat offence, with possible imprisonment of 1 day to 1 month.

## Pedagogical constraints and controls

Home education must enable the child to reach a level of studies equivalent to that of schools organised or subsidised by the Community, and must not promote values manifestly incompatible with the *European Convention on Human Rights*. The Code prescribes no curriculum, subjects, textbooks, or timetable; the constraint operates indirectly, through the *socles de compétences*, the *tronc commun*, and the external certification framework against which the child's progress is measured.

The certification sequence is in transition and should be verified by cohort. Current guidance requires EAD pupils to sit the **CEB** (*Certificat d'études de base*) by the school year in which they reach 12, and the external examination for the **CTC** (*Certificat du tronc commun*) by the school year in which they reach 15. Older or transitional cohorts may still be affected by the former CE1D / CE2D framework and by Jury pathways, including routes to the **CESS** around the end of compulsory-education age. Failure to obtain the certification applicable to the cohort at the required age triggers, absent derogation or dispensation, compulsory enrolment in a recognised school. The final cohort-by-cohort transitional position should be checked against current FWB guidance.

The *Service général de l'Inspection* monitors the level of studies. Inspections may occur at any time with at least one month's notice; two are mandatory during the primary cycle, in the calendar years of the child's 8th and 10th birthdays. Assessments are written, conducted in FWB premises (with adjustment for mobility difficulties linked to health or disability), and require parental participation and the production of pedagogical materials, including the *plan individuel de formation* where one exists. Two consecutive negative assessments, refusal of inspection, or an unjustified absence (a ground introduced in 2019) trigger compulsory enrolment. Appeals are lodged by registered post within 15 days; the Commission rules within one month. Children re-entering school after a negative outcome must remain enrolled for at least one full school year before EAD may be resumed.

## Recent developments

| Instrument | Effect |
| - | - |
| Draft *arrêté du Gouvernement de la Communauté française*, reported as adopted in first reading in early May 2026 | Strengthens upfront pedagogical documentation: detailed pedagogical project and full-year training plan at declaration; reinforced attention to disorders, disabilities, school phobia, and dropout. Implements the *Déclaration de politique communautaire 2024-2029*, which aims to reduce recourse to EAD. Systematises a PIF logic already present in derogation and inspection practice. |
| Tronc commun / certification transition | EAD certification milestones are being reframed around the CEB and *certificat du tronc commun*; cohort-by-cohort transitional details should be verified against current FWB guidance. |
| *Décret du 24 février 2022* | Amends Art. 1.7.1-12 of the Code, refining the EAD definition and the declaration mechanism. |
| *Décret du 3 mai 2019 portant les livres 1er et 2 du Code* | Codifies the substantive EAD regime into Art. 1.7.1-12 to 1.7.1-24 and abrogates the operative EAD provisions of the 2008 decree. |
| *Décret du 3 mai 2019 portant diverses dispositions en matière d'enseignement obligatoire et de bâtiments scolaires* | Introduces unjustified absence at inspection as an exit trigger, and the regime governing return to recognised education. |

The 2026 EAD-specific reform converges with the 2025 Flemish package on procedural tightening, increased pedagogical formalisation, and more systematic escalation in case of non-cooperation.

# Unschooling in the German-speaking Community of Belgium

## Context

The German-speaking Community of Belgium (*Deutschsprachige Gemeinschaft*, DG ; *Ostbelgien*) recognises home education (*Hausunterricht*) under a regulated registration, individual work-plan, inspection, and examination regime. It is therefore open in principle, but not compatible with curriculum-free unschooling.

## Home education

Home education is governed by Articles 93.47 to 93.62 (*Kapitel VIIIQUATER – Hausunterricht*) of the *Dekret vom 31. August 1998 über den Auftrag an die Schulträger und das Schulpersonal sowie über die allgemeinen pädagogischen und organisatorischen Bestimmungen für die Regel- und Förderschulen* (the *Grundlagendekret*). It is organised under a single regime — there is no Flemish-style distinction between individual and collective home education — and parents bear full pedagogical and financial responsibility. *Hausunterricht* is not subsidised in any form.

### Substantive conditions

Home education must:

- meet the requirements of the *schulisches Gesellschaftsprojekt* (framework societal project for education);
- enable the child to reach the competence level defined by the *Rahmenpläne* (framework curricula) and *Entwicklungsziele* (developmental objectives) adopted by the *Parlament der Deutschsprachigen Gemeinschaft*, and, where applicable, by the relevant *Lehrpläne*;
- take place in an appropriate environment;
- be delivered in one of the three official languages of instruction — German, French, or Dutch. Where German is the language of instruction, French must also be taught as the first foreign language; A2 level is expected by the end of sixth primary year.

The framework does not prescribe a fixed timetable or textbook requirements, and imposes no diploma or language-proficiency requirement on the parent or tutor. The constraint operates through alignment with the *Rahmenpläne*, the submitted work plan, the *Lernstandserhebungen*, and the external examination framework — together leaving room for individualised teaching methods but excluding curriculum-free unschooling.

### Procedural conditions

- A registration (*Anmeldung zum Hausunterricht*) must be filed with the *Ministerium der Deutschsprachigen Gemeinschaft*, *Fachbereich Ausbildung und Unterrichtsorganisation* in Eupen, at the latest three working days before the start of the school year, and must be renewed annually. According to the Minister's 2024 answer, the administration cannot refuse registration as such; control is exercised through the substantive, inspection, and examination regime.
- The registration must be accompanied by: a residence certificate not older than two months attesting domicile in the German-speaking Community; copies of the parents' and child's identity documents; the last school report, if any; the address where home education is to be provided; and an individual work plan (*individueller Arbeitsplan*) for each child.
- The work plan must set out the perspective on the child's learning processes and contain, at minimum, the time planning and the developmental objectives and competences to be reached per subject, grounded in the *Rahmenpläne* in force. Templates by age level are made available on the Ostbelgien Bildung portal.
- Mid-year switches from school to home education are permitted, with notification to the administration no later than the date of the change.
- Documents not in German, French, or Dutch must be accompanied by a sworn translation.
- Home-educated children are subject to compulsory school-medical examination by *Kaleido-Ostbelgien* from age 6 (and may not be exempted on the ground that they are home-educated).

### Examination obligations

Registration entails participation in the external examinations (*schulexterne Prüfungen*) before the *Prüfungsausschüsse der Deutschsprachigen Gemeinschaft*. Three milestones must be passed:

- *Abschlusszeugnis der Grundschule* (primary leaving certificate) — first compulsory external milestone, normally in the school year in which the child has turned 11 before 1 January; usually administered in June;
- *Abschlusszeugnis der Unterstufe der Sekundarschule* (lower secondary leaving certificate);
- *Abschlusszeugnis der Oberstufe der Sekundarschule* (upper secondary leaving certificate, *Abitur*).

For the secondary certificates, candidates may split subjects across several sessions. Examinations are offered primarily in German; on request, candidates may sit in French (or, in principle, Dutch), though in practice very few do so.

The 2024–2025 registration form also requires participation in regular *Lernstandserhebungen* (learning-level assessments). These have been administered annually to all pupils from the 1st to the 5th year of primary school since school year 2024–2025, and are framed by the Minister as a means of detecting competence gaps earlier than the primary-leaving examination.

### Re-enrolment triggers — September 2025 tightening

Under Art. 93.61 of the *Grundlagendekret* as previously in force, parents had to re-enrol their home-educated child where the child had failed the primary-leaving examination twice, or had not sat it by the year in which the child turned 14. The two-failure rule allowed late starters to retake at 15, occasionally producing 15- and 16-year-olds re-enrolled without a primary leaving certificate.

With effect from 1 September 2025, a young person must be re-enrolled *ex officio* if the primary-leaving certificate has not been obtained in the year they turn 14, regardless of how many times the examinations have been taken (an exemption procedure is available). The same principle applies to the lower-secondary leaving certificate, which must be obtained by age 17. The change shifts the trigger from repeated failure or non-sitting to non-obtainment of the certificate by the statutory age.

### Inspection

The *Schulinspektion* in principle conducts an annual inspection at the Ministry in Eupen, with newly registered families seen as quickly as possible. The work the child has produced is reviewed against the work plan and the *Rahmenpläne*, and social and cultural participation is considered. Deficiencies trigger a follow-up inspection within four months; persistent deficiencies result in referral to the *Hausunterrichtskommission* (Home Education Commission), which decides whether and under what conditions home education can continue and may order re-enrolment. Where the child's welfare appears at risk, the inspectorate may notify *Jugendhilfe* and the *Staatsanwaltschaft* directly after the first inspection.

### Residence and cross-border use

Foreign families resident in the German-speaking Community are subject to the same regime as Belgian nationals. The nationality data suggest significant cross-border use by German families, consistent with parents citing limited home-education options in their country of origin. A residence certificate from one of the nine DG municipalities (less than two months old) is a condition of registration.

## Recent legislative activity

| Instrument | Effect |
| - | - |
| *Dekret 2025 über Maßnahmen im Unterrichtswesen* (in force 1 September 2025) | Replaces the "twice failed / not sat by 14" re-enrolment trigger with *ex officio* re-enrolment where the primary-leaving certificate has not been obtained by the year the child turns 14 (lower-secondary by 17), regardless of attempts. |
| *Dekret vom 22. Juni 2020 über Maßnahmen im Unterrichtswesen* | Lowered compulsory age from 6 to 5 in line with the federal *Wet van 23 maart 2019*, extending the *Hausunterricht* regime to age 5; the third-year kindergarten *Entwicklungsziele* must be reflected in the work plan. Introduced compulsory *Kaleido-Ostbelgien* school-medical examinations for home-educated children from age 6, removing a prior exemption. |
| *Dekret vom 20. Juni 2016 über Maßnahmen im Unterrichtswesen* | The principal post-1998 reform. Created the *Hausunterrichtskommission*, formalised the inspection escalation pathway, expanded the documentary requirements at registration, and introduced the possibility of forced re-enrolment. |

Minister Jérôme Franssen confirmed in his answer to written question Nr. 15 of 31 October 2024 that the organisation of *Hausunterricht* remains under thorough review and that further amendments to the *Grundlagendekret* may follow.

## Statistics

Source: answer of Minister Franssen to written question Nr. 15 of 31 October 2024 (K. Elsen, ProDG); data current as of early November 2024. As of May 2026, no later official statistics have been located.

| School year | Pupils in home education |
| - | - |
| 2024–2025 | 109 (4 kindergarten, 76 primary, 29 secondary) |
| 2019–2020 | 54 |

In 2024–2025, the breakdown was: 99 in German, 10 in French, 0 in Dutch; 51 male, 58 female; 22 Belgian, 82 German, 3 French, 1 Romanian, 1 Ukrainian. Inspection appointments held: 70 in 2023–2024, 62 in 2022–2023. *Hausunterrichtskommission* cases: 4 in 2023–2024, 3 in 2022–2023.

Reasons reported by parents included limited home-education options in the country of origin, health concerns, special needs, individualised competence development, religious or philosophical convictions, and short-term pre-school arrangements before return to a foreign school system.

This increase, together with the reported rise in pupils with special needs, provides the immediate context for the 2025 tightening.

# Unschooling in Flanders

## Context

Flanders recognises parental choice of home education, but the regime is conditional throughout : on annual declaration, on substantive aims, on examination milestones, on inspection, and — since the 2025 reform package — on tightened procedural requirements at the point of declaration.

## Compulsory education

Compulsory education runs from age 5 to 18 — thirteen years. Full-time compulsory education ends at 15 ; from 15 to 18 part-time pathways combining work and study are permitted.

The Flemish authorities are explicit that this is compulsory *education*, not compulsory *schooling* :

> Education in Belgium is compulsory, but children do not need to go to school to comply with this obligation. Instead of sending your child to school, you can also opt for home education.
>
> — [Flanders.be, *Home education for children of compulsory schooling age*](https://www.vlaanderen.be/en/home-education-for-children-of-compulsory-schooling-age)

This distinction is also reflected in Art. 25 §1 of the *Decreet basisonderwijs van 25 februari 1997* : *« Ouders kunnen hun kinderen onderwijs laten volgen in een school of ze kunnen kiezen voor huisonderwijs. »*

## Home education

Home education (*huisonderwijs*) is defined in Art. 3, 24° of the *Decreet basisonderwijs* as instruction given to children of compulsory-school age whose parents have decided not to enrol them in a school recognised, financed or subsidised by the Flemish, French or German-speaking Community. It is organised under two regimes :

- **Individual home education** — the parent teaches the child or hires a private tutor.
- **Collective home education** — the child attends a private (non-recognised) school, or several parents organise instruction jointly. Pupils enrolled in a Belgian private school are exempt from filing the declaration ; the school does so on their behalf.

### Substantive conditions

Under Art. 26bis/1 of the *Decreet basisonderwijs* and Art. 110/29 of the *Codex Secundair Onderwijs van 17 december 2010*, home education must aim to :

- develop the child's personality, talents, and mental and physical skills ;
- teach respect for human rights and fundamental freedoms ;
- teach respect for the child's cultural values and those of others ;
- prepare the child for an active life as an adult.

The legislator prescribes neither a fixed curriculum nor compulsory subjects, textbooks or timetable. The constraint operates indirectly, through the *minimumdoelen* and examination framework that home education must realistically prepare the child to meet.

### Procedural conditions

- A *verklaring van rechten en plichten inzake huisonderwijs* — the formal title from the 2025-2026 school year onwards — must be submitted to AGODI (*Agentschap voor Onderwijsdiensten*) no later than the third school day of September. The declaration must be renewed annually.
- Switching from school to home education mid-year is not freely available. The ordinary declaration deadline is the third school day of September ; later declaration is permitted only in specified cases, including for children already accompanied by a CLB, where the CLB does not lodge a reasoned objection within ten working days of being informed of the declaration (Art. 26bis/1 *Decreet basisonderwijs* ; Art. 110/29 §2 *Codex Secundair Onderwijs*).
- From the 2025-2026 school year onwards, the declaration must designate a CLB (*Centrum voor Leerlingenbegeleiding*) within the child's province. Under Art. 36 §3 of the *Decreet leerlingenbegeleiding*, parents and pupils may not object to the systematic contacts themselves, but may object to their execution by a specific CLB staff member. The implementing *Besluit van de Vlaamse Regering tot operationalisering van de leerlingenbegeleiding* (Art. 8 §3) provides that in that case the contact may be performed by another CLB staff member or by a healthcare professional outside the CLB, subject to the statutory 90-day and reporting requirements.
- The declaration must specify the language of instruction, the educational objectives pursued, how the instruction is matched to the child's learning needs, the sources and learning materials to be used, and the manner in which the child's social participation (*sociale participatie*) is pursued (Art. 26bis/1 §1 *Decreet basisonderwijs* ; Art. 110/29 §1, 6°/1 *Codex Secundair Onderwijs*).

### Examination obligations

Children in home education must pass exams at the *Examencommissie van de Vlaamse Gemeenschap* :

- Register with the *Examencommissie Basisonderwijs* at the latest in the school year in which the child turns 11 before 1 January.
- Obtain a *getuigschrift basisonderwijs* (certificate of basic education) at the latest in the school year in which the child turns 13 before 1 January.
- Register with the *Examencommissie Secundair Onderwijs* at the latest in the school year in which the child turns 15 before 1 January.
- Obtain a certificate for the first cycle of secondary education at the latest in the school year in which the child turns 16 before 1 January.

Failure to meet any of these deadlines triggers compulsory enrolment in a school.

### Inspection

The *Onderwijsinspectie* may inspect home education to verify that the substantive conditions are met. Two consecutive negative assessments, or refusal of inspection, trigger compulsory enrolment in a school.

### Foreigners

Foreign families resident in Flanders are subject to the same home-education regime as Belgian nationals. Children enrolled in recognised schools of another Belgian Community, in European schools, or in eligible international schools may fall outside the *verklaring* requirement where AGODI can verify enrolment directly or where the parent files the required proof. Children attending a recognised school abroad file an *inschrijvingsbewijs* in lieu of the *verklaring*.

## Recent legislative activity

| Instrument | Effect |
| - | - |
| *Decreet van 18 juli 2025 over de lerenden en de organisatie in het onderwijs* (BS 08/08/2025) | Introduces new data-processing provisions for AGODI in relation to home-educated pupils (Art. X/3.1, *Codificatie sommige bepalingen voor het onderwijs van 28 oktober 2016*) ; underpins the tightened *verklaring* regime from the 2025-2026 school year. |
| *Decreet van 13 juni 2025 tot wijziging van het decreet basisonderwijs van 25 februari 1997* (BS 27/06/2025) | Amends the framework for *minimumdoelen*, relevant because home education is assessed against the child's right to education and the examination and certificate pathway. |
| *Decreet van 17 mei 2024 — Onderwijsdecreet XXXIV* (and related instruments through 2024) | Refines the joint *verklaring* mechanism for collective home education where children share an instructional address but are domiciled elsewhere. |

The 2025 tightening package, implemented through the 18 July 2025 decree and accompanying ministerial communications, also reframes how non-cooperation with the *Onderwijsinspectie* is handled : negative assessment may follow more quickly, and escalation to the public prosecutor or to social services has been signalled as more systematic.

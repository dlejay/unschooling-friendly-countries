# Unschooling in Oman

## Compulsory education

Royal Decree No. 31/2023, which entered into force on 22 May 2023, makes
basic education compulsory for every child from the age of 6.

A parent or guardian must enrol the child in the first grade of basic
education and ensure regular attendance until the child completes that
stage. A student may remain in basic education until completing it or
reaching the age of 17, subject to an exception for certain medically
certified health conditions.

The law therefore defines compulsory education by reference to completion
of the basic-education stage, rather than as a fixed period of “10–11
years”.

## Home education

Article 46 does not establish a general parental right to homeschool.
Instead, it provides that the Ministry of Education may permit home study
or distance learning for students whose circumstances require them not to
attend school regularly, subject to the applicable regulations.

The permission is discretionary and Ministry-controlled. The Law itself
does not expressly list serious illness, geographical remoteness or any
other particular circumstance as an automatic ground for approval.

Consequently, parents cannot ordinarily withdraw a child from school and
provide independent, interest-driven unschooling. Home study is available
only where the Ministry authorises it as an alternative means of delivering
the child's formal education.

## Enforcement

Where a parent or guardian fails to enrol a child or ensure regular
attendance, the authorities must first follow the procedures prescribed by
the regulations.

If the parent does not correct the situation within 14 days after those
procedures have been completed, or continues to permit non-attendance
without an acceptable excuse, the penalty is:

- imprisonment for between 1 and 3 months;
- a fine of between 500 and 1,000 OMR; or
- either of those penalties.

The parent is not punishable where the school administration failed to
notify them of the child's non-attendance in accordance with the prescribed
procedures.

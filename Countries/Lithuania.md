# Unschooling in Lithuania

## Compulsory education

Compulsory education comprises pre-primary, primary and basic education and continues until age 16. Since 1 January 2023, pre-primary education (priešmokyklinis ugdymas) begins in the calendar year in which the child turns 5 by 30 April ; parents may defer it, but not beyond the calendar year in which the child turns 6.

## Unschooling

Family education (ugdymasis šeimoje) was legalised by Law Nr. XIII-2593 of 3 December 2019, which inserted Article 31¹ into the Education Law ; the provisions entered into force on 1 June 2020, and the first children could be educated in the family from 1 September 2020. According to Article 31¹ :

> 1. At the request of the child and his/her parents (guardians, carers), the child may be educated in the family within the framework of pre-primary,
>    primary, basic and secondary education. In the case of family based education, the parents (guardians, carers)
>    or the child between the ages of 14 and 18 and the school of their choice enter into an educational agreement.
>    Family education is equivalent to school education.

The implementing rules are established by Government resolution Nr. 504 of 20 May 2020 (Ugdymosi šeimoje įgyvendinimo tvarkos aprašas). Under the current rules :

- The child must be educated according to the applicable national general curriculum (bendrosios programos) of pre-primary, primary, basic or secondary education. The family may choose methods, materials and learning environments, but must enable the child to attain the outcomes prescribed by the relevant curriculum.
- To begin family education on 1 September, the application and supporting documents are submitted to an authorised school during the annual period prescribed by the Aprašas — 15 April to 1 June (point 8). In practice, families should follow the timetable published by the assisting school : some municipalities (Vilnius, Kaunas, Telšiai) accept applications from 1 March, while others keep to 15 April – 1 June.
- The municipality publishes a list of schools authorised to assist families ; a learning agreement with one of these schools is mandatory. For a child under 14, the parents (guardians) apply ; a child aged 14 to 18 applies personally, with the written consent of a parent (carer).
- Conditions include : no child-rights violations established against the adults the child lives with during the previous two years, and no case-management process or minimum/medium supervision measures affecting the child during that period ; a home visit assessing learning conditions ; and the child's own expressed wish (family education is not possible against the child's will).
- Learning progress and achievements (what the child studied, what he learned, in what environments) must be recorded at least once every two weeks in the school's electronic diary (point 11.4.3).
- The school assesses the child's learning achievements and socialisation periodically, generally twice per school year, and provides consultations and textbooks.

Because the child must follow the national curriculum and demonstrate the prescribed outcomes, Lithuanian family education permits substantial flexibility in method and pacing, but does not legally permit wholly interest-driven unschooling.

## Statistics

| School year | Homeschoolers |
| ----------- | ------------- |
| 2022–2023   | 263           |
| 2021–2022   | 113           |
| 2020–2021   | 64            |

In 2022–2023, 221 of the 263 children were enrolled through non-state schools (ŠMSM data via LRT). No official national figure for later school years was located in this review ; the Ugdymo šeimoje asociacija has reported "about 300" children in recent years (approximate).

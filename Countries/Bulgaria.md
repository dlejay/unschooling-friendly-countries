# Unschooling in Bulgaria

## Compulsory education

According to the Pre-school and School Education Act,

> **Art. 8.** (1) (Amended – State Gazette, issue 82 of 2020) Pre-school education shall be compulsory from the school year beginning in the year in which the child turns 4 years of age, with parents choosing one of the forms under Art. 67, and the state and municipalities undertaking to ensure conditions for the enrolment of children in kindergartens and pre-school education groups.
>
> (2) School education shall be compulsory until the age of 16 and shall begin from the school year beginning in the year in which the child turns 7 years of age.
>
> (3) School education may also begin from the school year beginning in the year in which the child turns 6 years of age, at the parent's discretion and subject to school readiness certified under the conditions and procedures set out in the state educational standard for pre-school education.
>
> (4) The start of school education may be deferred by one school year where the child's health condition does not permit entry into grade I, as established under the procedure set out in the state educational standard for pre-school education.

## Homeschooling

Since the 2015 Pre-school and School Education Act, home-based education has been possible in Bulgaria only under the independent form of education.

Article 112 provides:

> (3) The independent form shall include self-study and examinations for determining annual grades in the subjects of the school curriculum.
>
> (4) In the independent form of education, the school curriculum for day or evening form shall apply.

### Approval process

For pupils of compulsory school age, independent education at parental request is not automatic.
It is organised after a decision of an expert commission at the relevant Regional Education Department and is monitored each school term.

### Assessment

Students in the independent form must sit examinations for annual grades in the subjects of the school curriculum.
If a pupil of compulsory school age does not achieve the required competences, the law provides for continuation in another form of education.

### Applicability to foreigners

The compulsory education obligation applies to all residents.
No legal exemption for expats was found.
Foreign families appear to be subject to the same school-linked independent form of education.

# Unschooling in Vietnam

## Compulsory education

Under Article 14 of the Law on Education No. 43/2019/QH14, primary education was the only compulsory level of education.
Law No. 123/2025/QH15, adopted by the National Assembly on 10 December 2025, amends the Law on Education and makes lower secondary education compulsory in addition to primary education.
The amending law entered into force on 1 January 2026, but under its Article 2(2) the amendment of Article 14 took effect on 1 July 2026.
Compulsory education therefore covers, since 1 July 2026, the nine-year basic-education stage, from the first grade (entrance age 6) to the ninth grade.

## Unschooling

Article 14 of the Law on Education (2019) provides :

> 3. All citizens in the prescribed age groups have the obligation to learn in order to obtain the purpose of universalization of education and completion of compulsory education.
>
> 4. Families and guardians have the responsibility to create conditions for family members in the prescribed age groups to learn in order to obtain the purpose of universalization of education and completion of compulsory education.

According to UNESCO :

> The Law on Education (2019) states that all citizens must complete compulsory education, and guardians are responsible for creating the conditions for family members of a certain age to study (Article 14). However, the Law on Education does not specify that homeschooling is forbidden. Further, unofficial sources indicate that homeschooling exists in Viet Nam.

The UNESCO profile describes the law as it stood before the 2025 amendment.
The obligations set out in Article 14 are framed in terms of citizens and attach to learning and to the completion of compulsory education.
Under Article 34 of the Law on Education (2019), completion of the primary education programme was certified in the student’s school record by the principal of his or her school, and the lower secondary diploma was awarded by the head of the specialised agency in charge of education under the district-level People’s Committee.
Under Article 45, a trainee who completed the lower secondary education programme through continuing education was likewise awarded the diploma by the head of that agency.
Law No. 123/2025/QH15 abolishes the lower secondary diploma ; learners who complete the lower secondary education programme have this completion confirmed in their school records by the school principal or the head of the educational institution.
In 2015, a leader of the Ministry of Education and Training stated to the press that the Law on Education contained no form of home study, the delivery of general education being entrusted to educational institutions.
The same official noted that continuing education comprises work-study, distance learning, and guided self-study as forms leading to qualifications of the national education system.
In 2017, Nguyễn Minh Thuyết, editor-in-chief of the general education curriculum programme, stated that parents not sending their children to school was inconsistent with Article 18 of the Law on Universal Primary Education and with Article 11(3) of the Law on Education then in force.
Article 26(2) of Decree No. 130/2021/NĐ-CP, in force since 1 January 2022, provides :

> 2. Phạt tiền từ 1.000.000 đồng đến 2.000.000 đồng đối với hành vi cản trở việc đi học của trẻ em.

This provision imposes an administrative fine of 1,000,000 to 2,000,000 đồng for the act of obstructing a child’s schooling.

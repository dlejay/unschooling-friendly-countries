# Home education in Åland

## Context

Åland is the autonomous, Swedish-speaking province of Finland. Education falls within Åland’s legislative competence under § 18 of the self-government act (1991:71), so Åland has its own primary-school legislation rather than simply applying Finland’s national Basic Education Act ; the relevant statute is the Landscape Act (2020:32) on childcare and primary school, in force since 1 January 2021.

Home education (*hemundervisning*) is open to all guardians of läropliktiga (compulsory-education-aged) children domiciled on Åland, with no prior permission required — only an annual notification. However, the regime is curriculum-bound : the guardian is responsible for ensuring that the child acquires the knowledge and skills set out in *Landskapet Ålands läroplan för grundskolan*, and the municipality assesses progress once per term. If, at the end of a school year, progress shows serious deficiencies, the municipality may terminate home education and require attendance at a grundskola.

Notification must be made *läsårsvis* (each school year) in writing to the home municipality, with a teaching plan attached. Since 1 January 2024, Mariehamn’s *Tillsynsenhet för Ålands hemundervisning* has handled the supervision service for Åland’s municipalities, although local arrangements for where assessments take place may vary. The deadline for filing the annual notification is 10 August (or as soon as possible after a mid-year move to Åland).

## The statute

Landskapslag (2020:32) om barnomsorg och grundskola, del III, 6 kap. *Hemundervisning*, §§ 27–29 (consolidated text on Ålex, including amendments through 2020/52 and 2020/72) — translation below is unofficial.

> **§ 27.** Home education means that the guardian is responsible for ensuring that a child whose hemort is on Åland under the Home Municipality Act fulfils the läroplikt under § 1 of part III. The guardian is responsible for ensuring that the child acquires the knowledge and skills set out in the curriculum for the grundskola.
>
> **§ 28.** Guardians shall, each school year, notify the home municipality in writing that the läroplikt is being fulfilled through home education. The notification shall be accompanied by a plan setting out how the guardian will arrange the teaching so that the entire läroplikt is fulfilled. On change of municipality of residence, the guardian shall provide the new municipality with the most recent statement under § 29(1) of part III on the child’s development and progress in relation to the curriculum.
>
> **§ 29.** Where a child fulfils the läroplikt through home education, the child’s development and progress in relation to the curriculum shall be assessed by the municipality once per term. […] If the child’s progress at assessment is significantly below what can reasonably be expected, the municipality shall inform the guardians so that they can remedy the deficiencies during the coming term.
>
> If the child’s development and learning during a school year show serious deficiencies, and the municipality considers that the läroplikt is not being fulfilled through home education and that the guardian is not meeting the obligation to ensure the child fulfils the läroplikt, the municipality may decide to terminate the home education and require the child to fulfil the läroplikt in the grundskola or a substitute grundskola. […]

The termination power and the once-per-term assessment requirement in § 29 were added by amendment 2020/52 — this is the substance of what YLE and Åland’s homeschooling community refer to as the “2021 reform”.

## Practical consequences

The growth in homeschooled children on Åland has been driven mainly by Swedish families relocating, since home education is effectively unavailable in Sweden : YLE reported that in 2024, Åland registered 132 home-educated children, compared with 11 in 2012. The supervision regime has tightened correspondingly. Four families told YLE that the stepped-up supervision was the reason they had left, and some families described avoiding scrutiny by deregistering from any country at all (forfeiting child benefits and healthcare in the process), or by claiming to have moved back to Sweden.

Further measures have been floated but, as of the date of this review, not enacted : a prior-permission requirement for guardians wishing to homeschool, with an evaluation of their ability to educate their children ; and an increase in assessment frequency beyond once per term.

# Unschooling in Finland

## Compulsory education

According to the Compulsory Education Act :

> **2 §**
> **Start and end of compulsory education**
> 
> Children who reside permanently in Finland are subject to compulsory education. For the purposes of this Act, a child is considered to reside permanently in Finland if they have a municipality of residence in Finland as defined in the Municipality of Residence Act (201/1994).
> 
> […]
>
> Compulsory education ends when the student turns 18, or earlier if they have successfully completed a qualification referred to in the Matriculation Examination Act (502/2019) or in the Act on Vocational Education and Training (531/2017), or a corresponding qualification completed in Åland or abroad.

## Unschooling

According to the Basic Education Act :

> **Completion of Basic Education (30.12.2020/1216)**
> 
> A child subject to compulsory education must participate in basic education arranged in accordance with this Act or otherwise acquire knowledge corresponding to the basic education syllabus. The completion of basic education begins at the time laid down in section 2, subsection 2 of the Compulsory Education Act, unless a decision has been made for the child under section 27 of this Act to begin education at an exceptional time. The completion of basic education ends when the basic education syllabus has been completed, or at the latest when schoolwork ends in the school year during which the pupil turns 17. (30.12.2020/1216)

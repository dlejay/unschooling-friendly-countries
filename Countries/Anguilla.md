# Unschooling in Anguilla

## Compulsory education

Under section 117 of the Education Act (R.S.A. c. E26), a child enters compulsory school age at the beginning of the academic year if the child has already turned 5 or will turn 5 by 31 December of that academic year. Compulsory school age normally continues until the last day of the academic year in which the child turns 17, or ends earlier if the child obtains a certificate or diploma awarded in accordance with the Act.

## Unschooling

Home education is expressly permitted by Division 5 of Part 3 of the Education Act, but it is registered, planned and monitored. Section 25 provides :

> (1) A parent of a student may provide, at home, a home education programme for the student if the parent complies with this section and if the programme meets the goals and objectives set out in section 3(3).
>
> (2) The parent shall—
>
> (a) before the commencement of a home education programme for the student; and
>
> (b) on an annual basis, after such commencement, for as long as the home education programme is offered,
>
> register the student with the Chief Education Officer.
>
> (3) An educational plan for each student in a home education programme shall be prepared and provided to the Chief Education Officer subject to the following conditions—
>
> (a) the initial educational plan shall be prepared and provided to the Chief Education Officer not later than 3 months before the commencement of the home education programme;
>
> (b) the educational plan shall be for a minimum period of 3 academic years and shall cover every year of the home education programme;
>
> (c) the educational plan shall include a description of the learning activities for the student that will comply with the goals and objectives set out in section 3(3); and
>
> (d) the educational plan shall be based on the national curriculum established under Part 8.

The Chief Education Officer must assess the student's achievement on a regular basis (section 25(7)) and, under section 26, **shall terminate** the programme if it no longer meets the goals and objectives of section 3, or if the student fails to meet standards of achievement, as measured by achievement testing, comparable to those of students in public schools. Before termination, the parent must receive advice, recommendations and an opportunity to show cause (section 26(3)) ; a termination decision may be appealed to the Education Appeal Tribunal within 14 days (section 26(4)).

Interest-driven education is therefore not possible : the plan must be based on the national curriculum, and the programme may be terminated if it no longer meets the statutory goals and objectives or if the student fails to achieve standards comparable to those of students in public schools. An immediate start is not possible either, since the initial plan is due 3 months before commencement.

# Unschooling in Grenada

## Compulsory education

Compulsory school age is defined as any age from 5 years to 17 years inclusive, following the 2024 amendment to the Education Act, which came into force on 1 September 2024.

## Context

Home education is expressly permitted under section 113 of the Education Act : a parent of a child of compulsory school age may provide a home-education programme if the parent complies with the home-education division and the programme meets the goals and objectives set out in section 3(3).

The educational-plan conditions are set out in section 113(3) :

> (3) An educational plan for each student who is receiving home education must be prepared and provided
> to the Chief Education Officer and must comply with the following conditions—
>
> (a) the initial plan must be prepared and provided to the Chief Education Officer three months prior to the commencement
> of the home education programme;
>
> (b) the plan must be for a maximum period of three school years and set out details for each year;
>
> (c) the plan may be extended subject to the approval of the Chief Education Officer;
>
> (d) the plan must include a description of the learning activities for the student that will comply with the goals
> and objectives set out in section 3(3); and
>
> (e) the plan must be based on the national curriculum as established by the Minister under Part IX.

Parents must register the student with the Chief Education Officer before commencement and every year thereafter for as long as the programme is provided (section 113(2)).

The Chief Education Officer must provide for regular assessment of the student’s achievement, communicate the results to the parent, and advise the parent and recommend improvements if the student is not making reasonable progress (section 113(6)).

Foreign nationals living in Grenada are subject to the same rules.

## Trend

The Education (Amendment) Act 2024 amended the definition of compulsory school age by replacing “sixteen” with “seventeen”, with commencement on 1 September 2024. The available parliamentary PDF is the Bill as passed by the House of Representatives, not a fully consolidated post-amendment Act.

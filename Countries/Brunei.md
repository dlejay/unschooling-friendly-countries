# Unschooling in Brunei

## Compulsory school age

The original Compulsory Education Order, 2007 (Chapter 211, Revised Edition 2011) defined a "child of compulsory school age" as a child **above 6 and not yet 15**, and who satisfies the conditions for receiving primary and lower secondary education determined by the Permanent Secretary — historically corresponding to the primary and lower-secondary compulsory period.

The **Compulsory Education Act (Amendment) Order, 2023** (S 54/2023), in force from **1 January 2024**, amended that definition by replacing **6** with **5**, and by replacing "primary and lower secondary education" with "pre-school, primary and secondary education". The upper bound was not amended. The current compulsory age band is therefore children **above 5 and not yet 15**.

The same amendment also changed the definition of "Government school" so that it now covers institutions providing full-time **pre-school, primary or secondary education**, rather than only primary or lower secondary education.

The Ministry of Education's January 2024 implementation guidance specifies that children reaching the age of 5 by 31 December must register for pre-school admission from the start of the following year — administrative guidance which sits alongside, rather than within, the amended statutory wording.

## Statutory text (original 2007 Order, pre-amendment)

The original Chapter 211, before amendment by S 54/2023:

> 3.—(1) A child of compulsory school age who is —
>
> (a) born on or after 1st January, 2002 ;
> (b) a citizen of Brunei Darussalam ; and
> (c) residing in Brunei Darussalam,
>
> shall attend regularly as a pupil at a Government school.

The structural obligation — regular attendance at a *Government school*, subject to the statutory carve-outs in section 3(3) for justified absence, ministerial exemption under section 4, and attendance at a private educational institution registered under section 65(1) of the Education Act — is unchanged by the 2023 Amendment. The operative age threshold shifted from above 6 to above 5. The "born on or after 1 January 2002" wording is a 2007 commencement-era cohort clause and should not be read as the practical current admission cut-off.

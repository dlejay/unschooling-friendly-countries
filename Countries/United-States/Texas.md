# Unschooling in Texas

## Compulsory education

Texas compulsory attendance applies to children who are at least 6 years old as of 1 September of the current school year and continues until the child's **19th** birthday, unless the child is exempt under Tex. Educ. Code § 25.086 (Tex. Educ. Code § 25.085(b)). A child younger than 6 who has previously been enrolled in first grade is also subject to compulsory attendance. Kindergarten and pre-K are not compulsory ; however, once a child is voluntarily enrolled in pre-K or kindergarten, attendance becomes compulsory for that period (§ 25.085(c)).

## Home education

### According to CRHE
> **Private school:** Parents may operate a homeschool as a private school. Parents must provide instruction in good citizenship, math, reading, spelling, and grammar. There are no notification, parent qualification, instruction time, bookkeeping, or assessment requirements.

### News
In 2025, Texas enacted Senate Bill 2, creating the Texas Education Freedom Accounts (TEFA) programme, administered by the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts and funded with $1 billion for its first year.

The programme begins with the 2026–27 school year. Applications for that first year ran from 4 February to 31 March 2026. Accepted private-school students are eligible for $10,474 for 2026–27, while homeschooled students, or students otherwise not enrolled in public school, pre-K, or kindergarten, may be eligible to receive up to $2,000 annually.

This is therefore a significant school-choice development, but not a general $10,000-per-year homeschool voucher.

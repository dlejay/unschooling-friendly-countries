# Unschooling in Gabon

## Compulsory education

The applicable legislation contains inconsistent provisions concerning the
beginning of compulsory education.

Under the education framework law (_Loi n° 21/2011_, Article 2), education
and training in Gabon are compulsory, and access to them is guaranteed to
every young Gabonese or foreign resident aged 3 to 16.

The Child Code (_Loi organique n° 003/2018_, Article 51) provides more
specifically that pre-schooling (« préscolarisation ») is compulsory for
every child aged 0 to 5, while schooling (« scolarisation ») is compulsory
for every child aged 5 to 16 living in Gabonese territory. Read literally,
the Code therefore purports to require pre-schooling from birth, while the
education framework law identifies the relevant age range as 3 to 16.

## Unschooling

The Constitution promulgated on 19 December 2024 (_Loi référendaire
n° 002-R/2024_), which superseded the 1991 Constitution, provides in
Article 29 that the care and education of children constitute a natural
duty of parents, exercised “under the surveillance and with the help of
the State and other public authorities”.

Article 25 provides that parents have the right, “within the framework of
compulsory schooling” (« dans le cadre de l’obligation scolaire »), to
decide upon the moral and religious education of their children — a clause
carried over verbatim from Article 1, 16° of the 1991 Constitution.

Article 32 guarantees freedom of teaching (« liberté de l’enseignement »)
to all and expressly provides that any person may open a pre-school,
primary, secondary or higher educational establishment under conditions
fixed by law. It does not establish a right to educate one’s children at
home.

According to the Child Code (_Loi organique n° 003/2018_) :

> **Article 52** : Parents, guardians, institutions and any other person
> having custody or charge of the child have the right :
>
> – to choose the type of education to give the child ;
>
> – to choose the school conducive to the child’s development ;
>
> – to orient the child according to his or her abilities ;
>
> – to give the child a good education in the family milieu ;
>
> – to serve as a model for consolidating the child’s attainments.

> **Article 64** : Every child has duties toward himself or herself, the
> family, the State, society and the international community.
>
> Subject to the restrictions provided by the texts in force, the child
> notably has the duty :
>
> Toward himself or herself :
>
> – to remain in the family home and to leave it only with parental
> authorization ;
>
> – to be enrolled in school (« être scolarisé ») at least until the age
> limit of compulsory instruction.

Article 52’s reference to education in the family milieu is best read as
describing parents’ educational role alongside formal schooling, rather
than establishing home education as an alternative to it. Article 51
expressly mandates _scolarisation_, Article 64 requires the child to be
_scolarisé_, and Article 25 of the Constitution places parental
educational choice within the framework of compulsory schooling.

No statutory registration, authorization or exemption procedure for
ordinary home education has been identified. Home education therefore
does not appear to be legally available as a general parental choice.

# Unschooling in the Arab Republic of Egypt

## Compulsory schooling

According to the Law on Education 1981 :

> **Article 54**  
> “Basic education is a right for all Egyptian children who reach six years of age.  
> The State is committed to providing it over nine school years.  
> Parents or guardians are required to comply with this obligation.  
> Governors … shall issue the decisions necessary to organize and implement the obligation with respect to parents or guardians … and to distribute the children subject to the obligation among basic-education schools.  
> Where places are available, it is permissible to reduce the minimum age to five and a half years, provided class density is not violated.”
>
> **Article 55**  
> “Basic education aims at developing pupils’ abilities and aptitudes, satisfying their interests, and providing them with the necessary values, behaviors, knowledge, practical and vocational skills suited to the different environments and local conditions.”
>
> **Article 56**  
> “Organization of study in basic education shall aim at:  
> • strengthening religious and national education;  
> • linking education with productive work;  
> • strengthening ties with the local environment;  
> • achieving integration between theoretical and practical aspects of study;  
> • ensuring that education relates to pupils’ lives and social and economic realities.”
>
> **Article 57**  
> “An examination at the end of basic education shall be held at the governorate level in two sessions.  
> Pupils who pass this examination are granted the Certificate of Completion of Basic Education.  
> A pupil who completes the primary cycle and shows vocational inclination may complete the compulsory schooling period in preparatory vocational classes, vocational schools, or vocational training centers…”
>
> **Article 58**  
> “If a child does not enroll at the prescribed time or does not regularly attend without an acceptable excuse for ten consecutive or intermittent days, the school principal must notify the parent or guardian by registered letter…  
> If the child does not attend within one week from receipt of the letter, or repeats absence without acceptable excuse, the parent or guardian shall be considered in violation of this Law, and the penalties of Article 60 shall be applied.”
>
> **Article 59**  
> “The principals of basic-education schools and those delegated by the competent governor from the supervision and inspection authority shall have the capacity of judicial officers for enforcing the compulsory schooling requirement.”
>
> **Article 60**  
> “A parent or guardian shall be fined ten pounds if the child fails to attend school or drops out without acceptable excuse more than one week after receiving the warning letter…  
> The violation is repeated and the penalty multiplied for every continuous or repeated absence without acceptable excuse after warning.”

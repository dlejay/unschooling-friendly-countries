# Unschooling in Saint Kitts and Nevis
## Compulsory education
Compulsory education in Saint Kitts and Nevis covers children ages 5–16 (7 years
of primary + 5 years of secondary education). The upper bound of 16 is fixed by
section 26(1) of the Education Act ; the starting age of 5 is the age prescribed by
the Minister under that section.
## Context
According to the Education Act :
> (3) An educational plan for each student who is receiving home education shall be prepared and provided
> to the Chief Education Officer subject to the following conditions :
>
> (a) the initial educational plan shall be prepared and provided to the Chief Education Officer three months prior to the commencement
> of the home education programme;
>
> (b) the educational plan shall be for a minimum period of three school years and shall cover every year of the home education programme;
>
> (c) the educational plan shall include a description of the learning activities for the student that will comply with the goals
> and objectives set out in section 3(3) of this Act; and
>
> (d) the educational plan shall be based on the national curriculum as established by the Minister under Part 8 of this Act.

The compulsory‑attendance duty applies to ‘every child of compulsory school age’ (sections 26 and 36), and the entitlement to education is keyed to residence rather than nationality : section 15 refers to ‘every compulsory school‑age person who resides in the island’. The only nationality‑linked provision is free tuition, which is restricted to citizens of OECS Member or Associated Member States (section 16).
## Home‑Based Education Regulations 2011
Home education under section 80 is operationalised by the Education (Home‑Based Education) Regulations, 2011, made under the Minister’s general regulation‑making power in section 172 of the Act and published by the Ministry of Education. The published text is unnumbered — the ‘No. of 2011’ SRO field and the date of making are both left blank — so its formal commencement status is not certain ; absent a superseding gazetted version, it is the best available official text and should be treated as the applicable framework.

A programme must be registered before it operates (regs 4, 6). Notification, a written educational plan and a signed declaration of intent are submitted to the Chief Education Officer (regs 5, 16) : where no programme is yet registered, at least three months before the intended start but no later than 15 July ; for a continuing programme, by 15 August ; otherwise, at least 30 days before withdrawing a child of compulsory school age from a school. Late notification may be accepted where the Chief Education Officer considers a reasonable attempt to comply was made (reg 5(3)). Registration runs to the end of the school year and is renewable annually (reg 7).

Instruction may be given by an educator supervised by a certified teacher — defined as joint planning, at least one contact hour per week, and evaluation by that teacher — or by an educator whom the Chief Education Officer deems sufficiently qualified to teach unsupervised (reg 13). Average daily instructional time must be at least three hours (reg 10). The programme must cover a curriculum and instruction in the basic skills (science, mathematics, language arts, social studies, history, health science, reading and writing, spelling) for hours equivalent to the annual programme hours prescribed for private schools at the relevant grade, and must fulfil the Ministry’s curriculum standards (regs 3(2), 15(b)). The educator provides assessment samples and discusses the student’s progress with a principal or official twice during the school year (reg 15), maintains a portfolio and summative record (reg 17), and files an annual progress report (reg 18). The Chief Education Officer monitors each programme and may require an improvement plan, and ultimately terminate registration, where a student is not making satisfactory progress commensurate with his age and ability (regs 21, 22, 25).

Within those requirements the regime is deliberately flexible : the educational plan need not set out a full scope and sequence (reg 16(4)) ; a programme may concentrate on a selected subset of the goals of education rather than all of them (reg 16(5)) ; and an educator may not be required to include material that conflicts with, or to exclude material consistent with, his or her conscientious beliefs (reg 16(7)).

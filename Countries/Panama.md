# Unschooling in Panama

## Compulsory education

In Panama, the first level of instruction, _Educación Básica General_ (General Basic Education), is compulsory (Constitution, Article 95). It lasts 11 years : 2 years of preschool, 6 of primary education and 3 of _premedia_, normally covering ages 4 to 15. The second level, _Educación Media_ (3 years), is not compulsory.

## Home education

Home education (_educación en casa_) was established by Law 245 of 13 October 2021, published in Gaceta Oficial No. 29398-B of 15 October 2021. It is not free-form homeschooling : the law establishes it as a distance-education modality, applicable to both _Educación Básica General_ and _Educación Media_, which may be offered by official or private schools. The law opens the modality to all students who meet its requirements, including students with special educational needs or high abilities.

Under the law, students must be enrolled in an educational centre designated by the Ministry of Education (Meduca). The designated centre must provide educational resources and printed learning guides, or an adequate technological platform, and the student's progress is subject to the two standard forms of assessment of the Panamanian system, qualitative and quantitative. The parent or guardian must undergo training in teaching methodology and personalised education, must give the student time for physical, sporting, cultural or recreational activity and evidence that participation, and must periodically submit to the centre evidence of the work completed at home. In 2022, Meduca communications also listed parental requirements including possession of a computer, an internet connection, a signed statement of responsibility, a mental-health certificate for the parent and a psychological report on the student issued by the psychological offices ; substantially the same list also appears in the legislative history of Bill 558.

The system therefore permits instruction to take place physically at home, but within a supervised, school-based distance-education framework. It does not permit autonomous, curriculum-free or interest-driven education.

The implementing regulations were issued by Executive Decree No. 45 of 29 April 2024, published in Gaceta Oficial No. 30030 of 14 May 2024. The decree provided for implementation on a transitional and experimental basis during the 2024–2025 school calendar, with participating official and private centres reviewed and validated through Meduca's regional directorates and Regional Technical Commissions. Article 38 of the decree provides that private schools may deliver the modality from the decree's entry into force, provided they satisfy the requirements it establishes.

## In practice

Implementation appears to remain incomplete. Press reporting from June 2025 indicated that many practical elements of the decree had not yet been put in place, and that families educating at home in Panama commonly enrol their children with foreign umbrella schools or platforms and subsequently have the resulting credentials validated by Meduca. In May 2024, the then Minister of Education, Maruja Gorday de Villalobos, stated that around 500 families in the country had been practising home education before the law was adopted.

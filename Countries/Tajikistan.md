# Unschooling in Tajikistan

## Compulsory education

General secondary education is compulsory. Under an amendment to the Law on Education made by Law № 2044 of 1 March 2024, pupils must, after completing basic general education (grade 9), continue either in general secondary school (grades 10–11) or, according to their wishes and abilities, in an institution of initial or secondary vocational education. Compulsory education therefore covers eleven years of schooling or a vocational pathway after grade 9. Obstructing a person’s receipt of general secondary education is a criminal offence (Criminal Code art. 164, amended in 2024).

## Home education

The Law on Education (22 July 2013, № 1004 ; latest amendment Law № 2228 of 17 December 2025) recognises a family form of instruction (семейная форма) as a generally available option, not one reserved to special categories.

Article 6(4) grants citizens of Tajikistan, foreign nationals and stateless persons the right to choose the educational institution and the form of instruction, expressly listing the family form (семейное) alongside daytime, evening, correspondence, inclusive, distance and external study. Article 16(5) provides that instruction in general-education institutions is organised in those same forms, including the family form, under a procedure determined by the authorised state body for education.

No located provision restricts the family form to particular categories such as illness, disability, giftedness or remoteness.

A separate provision (Article 6(8)) requires the state to organise free instruction at home for pupils who cannot attend school for prolonged periods for medical reasons. This medical home instruction is a distinct legal basis and should not be confused with the elective family form.

The Law makes execution of the state educational standards and curricula obligatory for all educational institutions ; the standards fix a compulsory minimum programme content, minimum and maximum teaching loads, and graduate-level requirements. Because the family form is a form of general education organised within general-education institutions (Article 16(5)), instruction under it must deliver the state standard ; it is therefore not a vehicle for interest-driven, child-led education.

The Law on Responsibility for the Education and Upbringing of the Child (20 June 2024, № 2049), which replaced the 2011 parental-responsibility law, requires parents to ensure the child receives an education, not to impede the child from receiving general secondary education, to monitor the child’s learning and to cooperate with the educational institution.

The exact modalities of the family form remain unclear. Article 16(5) frames it as a form in which instruction in general-education institutions is organised, under a procedure determined by the authorised state body. This suggests some form of institutional attachment, but the implementing procedure has not been located, so the enrolment mechanism, the role of the educational institution, assessment requirements and any grounds for refusal are undetermined.

## News

- March 2024 : Law № 2044 made continued education after grade 9 compulsory, in general secondary or vocational education ; Criminal Code art. 164 (obstructing receipt of general secondary education) was amended.
- June 2024 : Law № 2049 replaced the 2011 parental-responsibility law, extending responsibility to all participants in the educational process.
- The Ministry of Education and Science plans a transition to a 12-year school system from the 2029–2030 school year, with compulsory basic education extended to grade 10 ; the timetable has slipped repeatedly.

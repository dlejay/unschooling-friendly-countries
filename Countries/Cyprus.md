# Unschooling in Cyprus

## Compulsory education

Compulsory education extends until **completion of lower secondary education, around age 15**. The starting age is currently being lowered in stages : the former baseline was **4 years and 8 months** ; it fell to **4 years and 6 months in 2024/2025**, **4 years and 5 months in 2025/2026**, and **4 years and 4 months for 2026/2027**.

Cyprus is phasing the compulsory pre‑primary starting age down from the former 4y8m baseline toward age 4. The roll‑out is gradual, school year by school year, with full implementation expected by the **2031/2032** school year. A related but distinct policy goal — free pre‑school availability from age 4 — is targeted for around 2030 ; the two should not be conflated.

## Home education

Home education is not available as a parental choice in Cyprus. Cypriot law does not provide a general elective home‑education route outside the special‑needs / serious‑health‑problem framework.

That framework operates under the **Education and Training of Children with Special Needs Laws**, beginning with **Law 113(I)/1999 as amended**, and is restricted to :

- children with special educational needs ;
- children with serious health problems making school attendance difficult ;
- children with shorter‑term serious problems that temporarily prevent attendance.

This is not elective homeschooling in the Anglo‑American sense. It is **Ministry‑approved home instruction**, delivered by a **Ministry‑approved teacher**, within the **national curriculum** (with simplified versions of official exams to enable promotion to the next grade). Approval rests with the Ministry of Education, Sports and Youth.

The compulsory‑attendance rule applies to all children residing in Cyprus, including expatriates and foreign nationals. UNHCR Cyprus's public‑facing guidance states that **homeschooling is not permitted in Cyprus**. Relocation‑sector commentary sometimes suggests authorities have taken a less active stance toward expatriate families demonstrating serious educational provision, but such accounts are informal and non‑authoritative. Reports also exist of expatriate families being required to enrol their children in school or leave the country after attempting to home‑educate without approval. Families should not rely on informal tolerance.

## News

- **March 2024** : the House of Representatives passed an amendment to begin lowering the compulsory starting age toward 4. Implementation is staged year by year through to the 2031/2032 school year.

# Unschooling in Nepal

## Compulsory education

In Nepal, compulsory education extends to the end of the basic level (grades 1 to 8), and the framework applies to children who have completed four years but not completed thirteen years of age.

## Context

Under the Act Relating to Compulsory and Free Education, 2075 (2018), the duty to provide this compulsory education falls on the State (§6(1)).
“Basic education” is defined as school education from grade one to grade eight (§2(d)).
At least one year of early childhood development and education is provided after the age of four, before grade one (§2(i), §6(2)).

The Act defines compulsory education as the mandatory, regular study and completion of the basic level upon admission to a school or other alternative educational institution (§2(a)).
It places a corresponding duty on citizens :

> **Duty of the citizens:** (1) Every citizen shall have the duty to get education of the basic level.
>
> (2) Every citizen shall have the duty to send his or her children, who are of the age group of acquiring the basic education, to school regularly.

The Act contains no provision recognising parent-led home education as a means of discharging this duty.
The alternatives it recognises are institutional and regulated : state-provided alternative education for children with no convenient school (§14), non-formal and open schooling (§15), and traditional education through registered Gurukul, Gumba or Madarsa following the government curriculum (§16).

A guardian who does not enrol a school-age child, or who removes the child before completion of the basic level, may be deprived of facilities provided by the Local Level (§7(5)), after prior consultation and encouragement to send the child to school (§7(6)).
From 14 April 2028, a person who has not acquired basic education is disqualified from public service, from appointment or election to office in public, non-governmental or private institutions, and from incorporating or holding office in a company, firm, cooperative or non-governmental organisation, subject to the Act’s exceptions for persons who had already completed twelve years of age at commencement, persons already holding such positions or enjoying such facilities at commencement, and persons unable to acquire education because of weak health, multiple disability or a similar complex situation (§19).

The Act’s duties are framed in terms of citizens, and it provides no distinct home-education regime for foreign residents.

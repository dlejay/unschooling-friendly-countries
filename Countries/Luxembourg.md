# Unschooling in Luxembourg

## Compulsory education

Current range: ages 4–16 (12 years).
From September 2026: ages 4–18 (14 years), per Law of 20 July 2023.

## Homeschooling

Article 21 of the Act of 6 February 2009 states that:

> Parents who intend to homeschool their child must explain their motives in their application
> and request authorization from the school district inspector. Such authorisation may be limited in time.
> Homeschooling must be aimed at acquiring the skill bases defined by the study plan.

Authorization is annual, requires a detailed plan and criminal record extracts, and is subject to Ministry inspections (visits, interviews, portfolio, small tests).

### Expats/foreigners
 
Government is wary of homeschooling in languages other than Luxembourgish, German, or French.
Short-term expat families can homeschool relatively easily.
Long-term English-speaking residents face a harder path — language is the main barrier.
Allophone families have been refused in the past.

## Statistics

208 children authorized in 2023 (145 fundamental, 63 secondary) per Ministry data presented at parliamentary hearing of 25 July 2024.
Very small minority vs. ~111,000 school-age population.

## News

In 2023, the age of compulsory education was raised from 16 to 18 years old (takes effect September 2026).
 
Since 2024, a bill is being discussed to strengthen controls and unify the law on children older than 12 years old.
At the July 2024 parliamentary hearing, the Ministry stated it does not intend to promote homeschooling, cited risks of isolation and radicalization, and confirmed a separate bill on inspection of non-school teaching premises is also being prepared.
As of March 2026, neither bill has been submitted to Parliament.
 
New primary curriculum and French literacy option (ALPHA reform) rolling out from September 2026 — may shift what inspectors expect.

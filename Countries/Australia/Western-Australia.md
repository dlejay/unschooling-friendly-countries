# Unschooling in Western Australia

## Compulsory education

Western Australia’s compulsory education period runs from the beginning of the year in which a child turns five years and six months old, until whichever happens first : the end of the year in which the child turns seventeen years and six months old, or the day the child turns eighteen.

If a child satisfies the minimum requirements for secondary school graduation under the School Curriculum and Standards Authority Act 1997 before that point, the compulsory education period is deemed to end when those requirements are satisfied.

## Unschooling

According to the Education Act :

> **10. Ways in which s. 9 satisfied**
>
> (1) The duty imposed by section 9 is satisfied in respect of a child by—
>
> (a) the enrolment of the child at a school; or
>
> (b) the registration under section 48 of a parent as the child’s home educator and the continuation in effect of that registration.
>
> […]
>
> **47. Registration as child’s home educator, application for**
>
> (4) Subject to subsection (5), an application is to be made by the last Friday in February in the first year for which the parent wishes to be registered as the child’s home educator.

Registration is the legal basis for home education, and a parent’s responsibility for the child’s home education programme begins on the registration date stated on the certificate.

For a child already enrolled in a WA school, the Act provides a transition mechanism : the child is excused from attendance where a parent has applied, or intends to apply, for home educator registration, provided the principal is notified in writing as soon as practicable and in any case within three school days of the non‑attendance starting.
The application must then be made within fourteen days after the last day on which the child was recorded as attending, participating in the school’s educational programme, or otherwise complying with section 23.

Once registered, a home educator is subject to ongoing oversight : a home education moderator visits within three months of registration, and at least once every twelve months thereafter, to evaluate the education programme against the Western Australian Curriculum and Assessment Outline.

Also as explained on the official website of the Department of Education :

> As a home educator you need to develop a curriculum program which meets the requirements of School Curriculum and Standards Authority Act 1997.

This curriculum‑alignment requirement means the programme cannot be purely interest‑driven or unschooling‑led in legal terms ; even where a family uses child‑led methods day to day, the programme must remain capable of being evaluated against the WA Curriculum and Assessment Outline, which SCSA describes as the mandated curriculum framework.

## Statistics

| 2024 | 7,300         |

Note : the figures above are from the Home Education Association series, compiled from Australian Bureau of Statistics data, February 2025.
The Department of Education’s own annual reports use a different “students registered to receive home education” series, as at March each year, and should be preferred where exact Department figures are needed — reportedly 4,562 (2021), 6,151 (2022), 6,466 (2023), 7,325 (2024) and 7,526 (2025), though these were not independently verified against the source table for this entry.

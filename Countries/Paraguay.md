# Unschooling in Paraguay

## Compulsory education

Compulsory education in Paraguay is structured as a layered obligation spanning **Educación Inicial** (the preescolar year, age 5), **Educación Escolar Básica** (nine years, ages 6 to 14) and **Educación Media** (three years, ages 15 to 17) — thirteen school years in total, although some policy summaries describe the compulsory span more broadly.

The constitutional baseline remains Educación Escolar Básica, while later statutes extend the statutory guarantee and the corresponding obligation further, into both the preescolar year and educación media.

## Context

The compulsory scope of education in Paraguay is set across several instruments :

- The **Constitution of Paraguay (1992)**, Article 76, establishes the constitutional baseline :

  > **Article 76. Of the Obligations of the State**
  >
  > Elementary [básica] school education is obligatory.

- **Law N° 4088/2010** extends the guarantee of free education, and the compulsory scope, to **Educación Inicial** (the preescolar year, age 5) and to **Educación Media** (ages 15 to 17).

- **Law N° 5749/2017** (Carta Orgánica del Ministerio de Educación y Ciencias) reaffirms the State's duty to guarantee access to and completion of compulsory education, including educación media.

The General Law of Education (Ley N° 1264/1998) recognises parents as the « natural and primary agents » of their children's education. The parental right under Article 129 is framed as choosing an *institución educativa* aligned with the family's philosophical, ethical or religious convictions — not as choosing home education. Article 130 frames the corresponding parental obligation as ensuring that children receive compulsory school education.

## Legal status of home education

No ordinary statutory homeschooling pathway is apparent in Paraguayan law. The legal architecture appears to assume attendance at a recognised educational institution, and home education is not clearly contemplated as a distinct route to fulfilling the parental obligation.

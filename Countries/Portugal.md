# Unschooling in Portugal

## Compulsory education

The legal framework establishes 12 years of compulsory education, ordinarily spanning ages 6 to 18.

## Context

Individual and home education (« ensino individual » and « ensino doméstico ») are governed by Decreto-Lei n.º 70/2021, of 3 August, which repealed the earlier Portaria n.º 69/2019. Article 9 (Matrícula) provides :

> 3 — The application must be accompanied by the criminal-record certificate, in the case of individual teaching, and by the certificate of academic qualifications of the responsible educator, in accordance with the qualifications required under paragraphs 1 and 2 of Article 16.

and Article 12 (Protocolo de colaboração) :

> 2 — The collaboration protocol must include, in particular :
>
> e) The adoption of Portuguese as the language of instruction, without prejudice to parts of the curriculum being delivered in one of the foreign languages that form part of the national curriculum through the bilingual approach, provided that the following cumulative requirements are met :
>
> i) the responsible educator presents proof of linguistic proficiency in the relevant foreign language ;
>
> ii) the school of enrolment offers that educational provision.

The educational pathway must be organised in accordance with the national *Aprendizagens Essenciais* and the *Perfil dos Alunos à Saída da Escolaridade Obrigatória*, subject to portfolio-based monitoring and to certification by the school of enrolment.

The legal framework distinguishes two regimes. Under « ensino doméstico », the responsible educator — a family member or a person residing with the pupil — must hold at least a *licenciatura* (a bachelor’s degree), pursuant to Article 16(1). Under « ensino individual », instruction is delivered by a teacher qualified for the relevant level (Article 16(2)).

## News

- Decreto-Lei n.º 8/2025 (11 February 2025) amended Decreto-Lei n.º 70/2021 to align it with the inclusive-education regime (Decreto-Lei n.º 54/2018) and the national framework for external assessment (Decreto-Lei n.º 55/2018), extending to home-educated pupils with inclusive-education needs the support and assessment accommodations available in schools.
- Decreto-Lei n.º 62/2023 (July 2023) amended the rules governing examinations and the accommodations for pupils with special educational needs.

# Unschooling in Belarus

## Compulsory education

General secondary education is compulsory. Since 1 September 2022, this has required completion of all three stages of general secondary education — grades I to XI — rather than only the nine-year basic level. Children generally begin school at age 6.

## Home Education

Under the Education Code and its implementing rules, education at home is available only to pupils who, for medical reasons, temporarily or permanently cannot attend an educational institution. It is organised by an educational institution, follows the state educational programme and timetable, and requires the relevant medical conclusion.

This is not autonomous homeschooling or unschooling.

## News

A further tightening took effect on 1 September 2025 under Law No. 46-Z of 5 December 2024, which requires parents to ensure that minor children residing in Belarus receive general secondary education in educational institutions of the Republic of Belarus.

The Ministry of Education states that online or distance study at general-secondary level is not provided for in Belarus. In practical terms, this excludes foreign online or distance schooling as a substitute for Belarusian schooling for children physically resident in Belarus. Belarusian children living abroad may study where they reside, and foreign children residing in Belarus must study in Belarusian institutions.

# Unschooling in Antigua and Barbuda

## Compulsory education

Children aged 5 to 16 are of compulsory school age. Education is free up to age 18, but compulsory education runs only to age 16. During compulsory-school age, a child must either attend school or be covered by an approved home-education programme.

## Context

Home education is expressly permitted. The Education Act 2008 lets a parent choose home, private, or public schooling for their child (s. 21), and section 81 sets out the home-education regime : the parent must register the student with the Director of Education before the programme begins and annually thereafter, and must submit an educational plan.

Section 81(3) — as amended by the Education (Amendment) Act 2020, which repealed and replaced paragraphs (a) and (b) — currently reads :
> (a) The initial educational plan shall be prepared and provided to the Director of Education **three months or such shorter period as the Director deems necessary**, prior to the commencement of the home education programme;
>
> (b) The educational plan shall be for a **minimum period of one school year or such shorter period as the Director deems necessary**, and shall cover every year of the home education programme;
>
> (c) The educational plan shall include a description of the learning activities for the student that will comply with the goals and objectives set out in section 3(3) of the Education Act (2008); and
>
> (d) The educational plan shall be based on the **national curriculum** as established by the Minister under Part 8 of the Education Act (2008).

The June 2019 Curriculum Development Unit guidance still reproduces the original 2008 wording (three months’ notice, three-school-year minimum), so on points (a) and (b) it predates and is superseded by the 2020 amendment.

Children resident in Antigua and Barbuda who are of compulsory-school age are subject to the local compulsory-education framework ; no separate exemption for expatriate or non-citizen families has been identified.

## Statistics

| Year | Applications approved |
| ---- | --------------------- |
| 2020 | 8                     |

The only public figure is a September 2020, COVID-era snapshot : eight applications had been approved, with seven pending and two on hold ; the Ministry of Education described this as the highest number on record, attributing the rise to the COVID-19 pandemic. The same report identified the educational plan as the key required document and referred to planning for at least one year — consistent with the one-school-year minimum introduced by the 2020 amendment.

## Trend

Compulsory schooling ends at age 16, with free education available to age 18 ; the Education Act 2008 does not fix the compulsory age in the statute itself but empowers the Minister to set — and to raise or lower — it by order, subject to an affirmative resolution of Parliament. No order extending the upper limit beyond 16 has been identified. The Education (Amendment) Act 2020 points toward less procedural friction for home educators — shorter possible notice and plan periods at the Director’s discretion — but not toward curriculum-free unschooling : the plan must still cover the programme and remain based on the national curriculum.

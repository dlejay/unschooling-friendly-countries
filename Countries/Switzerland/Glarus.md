# Unschooling in Glarus
## Pending legislation
A Teilrevision of the *Gesetz über Schule und Bildung*, passed by the Landsgemeinde 2025, enters into force on 1 August 2026. The published cantonal implementation note describes the reform as concerning teacher working conditions, class-size rules, age relief, and competence allocation between canton and communes. It does not mention Art. 9 or the private-instruction regime, but the consolidated post-revision text should be re-checked once published.
## Compulsory education
Education in Glarus is compulsory for 11 years, comprising two Kindergarten years followed by primary and lower-secondary schooling. Children who have turned age 4 by 31 July become school-compulsory at the beginning of the following school year (Bildungsgesetz Art. 43 Abs. 1).
## Home education
Home education (*privater Einzelunterricht*) is legally possible only under a cantonal permission regime.
Under Art. 9 of the *Gesetz über Schule und Bildung*, legal guardians who want their school-age children to be taught individually, or to teach them themselves, require permission from the Department.
Permission is granted only if both statutory conditions are met : the instruction must be provided by persons holding a recognised, stage-appropriate teaching qualification, and it must guarantee education equivalent to that of the public schools.
The 2022 *Verordnung über den privaten Einzelunterricht und die Privatschulen*, together with the cantonal Mitteilung of 23 November 2022, confirms the same core approach : private instruction must be delivered by trained specialist staff and satisfy the principle of equivalence with public-school education. Some deviations in organisation or curriculum may be allowed, but the regime remains curriculum-bound and professionally supervised.
Unschooling is therefore not compatible with the Glarus regime.

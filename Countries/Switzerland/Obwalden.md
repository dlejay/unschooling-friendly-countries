# Unschooling in Obwalden

## Compulsory education
 
Obwalden frames the obligation as *Schulpflicht* (Bildungsgesetz, GDB 410.1).
 
- **Art. 56 Abs. 2 BiG** : ten years, starting with the obligatory kindergarten year, ending at most on completion of the Orientierungsschule.
- **Art. 68 BiG** : kindergarten entry the school year following the child’s fifth birthday ; one year mandatory, optional second year.
- **Art. 56 Abs. 4 BiG** : early release possible after nine school years or age 15.
- **Art. 23 Abs. 1 BiG** : guardians responsible for attendance.
- **Art. 129 BiG** : wilful absence punishable by detention or fine ; parents liable.
Discharge routes : public school, authorised private school (Art. 37), or private instruction under cantonal authorisation (Art. 40).
 
Art. 28 of the cantonal constitution allows parents to teach at home, but the BiG channels that right through a discretionary authorisation regime.
 
## Home education
 
The official cantonal position is explicit:

> Privatunterricht, ausserhalb von Privatschulen, ist im Kanton Obwalden nur in Ausnahmefällen möglich.

Under **Art. 40 BiG**, private instruction outside private schools is not allowed. It may be authorised only exceptionally where fulfilment of compulsory schooling is possible neither at a public school nor at a private school.

## News
 
In 2023 the Obwalden government began a revision of its Bildungsgesetz. The Vernehmlassung (mid-May to mid-August 2024) had 24 respondents, including one homeschooling family (« Kayser »), who argued that the reworked Art. 40 on Privatunterricht would contradict Art. 28 of the cantonal constitution.
 
The Kantonsrat adopted the Nachtrag on 22 May 2025. The SVP triggered a referendum ; on 30 November 2025 the electorate approved it with 57 % (turnout 52 %), majorities in all municipalities. The new Art. 40 reads : « Der Besuch von Privatunterricht zur Erfüllung der Schulpflicht ausserhalb von Privatschulen bedarf der Bewilligung des Kantons. » Substantively identical to the pre-reform wording ; the restrictive implementation practice remains.

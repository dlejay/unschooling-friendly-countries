# Unschooling in St Gall

## Compulsory education

In St. Gallen, as in most Swiss cantons, compulsory schooling lasts 11 years,
including two years of kindergarten (starting at age 4).

## Homeschooling

According to the *Volksschulgesetz* :
> Art. 117
>
> b) Granting
>
> 1. in general
>
> Permission will be granted if:
>
> a) School administration, professional management, organization and school premises ensure teaching that is equivalent to that of a public
> school and is designed for the long term;
>
> b) the obligatory subjects of the public school are taught.
>
> The Education Council can attach conditions to the approval in order to ensure the equivalence of the teaching.
>
> […]
>
> Art. 123
> Private One-to-One Lessons
>
> For private one-to-one tuition, the provisions of this law on private schools are applied appropriately.
>
> The Education Council grants the permit if the child’s education towards social competence (Gemeinschaftsfähigkeit) is also ensured.

## News

Local media have covered the slow emergence of homeschooling in SG.
A Tagblatt feature (Apr. 2025) describes how SG approved its first home-schooling requests
at the end of August 2022 and that the number of children taught at home has since grown
(especially in the Toggenburg region). The headcount has continued to rise : from 63 children
in 38 families (Dec. 2024) to 79 children in 47 families across eleven learning groups (Dec. 2025),
though the Schulaufsicht 2025 report notes considerable churn — families switching Lernorte,
groups splitting over pedagogical differences, and one Lernort closing after a single year.
The Education Council approves only cases meeting the group-instruction rule set out in the
Homeschooling St. Gallen association’s concept (June 2022 version) : at least 65–70 % of
teaching time in learning groups of no fewer than three children from three different families
per cycle, taught by a teacher holding a diploma for the relevant level.
Earlier coverage (2022) noted that three SG families prevailed before the administrative court
(*Verwaltungsgericht*) – the court found that the canton could not outright ban homeschooling
and must justify denials.
In 2023 one SG family won an appeal forcing the council to re-evaluate its case ;
after revising their request to meet the group-instruction criteria, the application was ultimately approved.
Likewise, two families whose original applications had been rejected resubmitted under the 2022 concept
and received approval.
The supervision framework was formalised in a *Detailkonzept* (issued 22 Nov. 2023) and amended
on 19 March 2025 (BRB 2025/47), chiefly covering the permanent-authorisation procedure, the
renewal of temporary authorisations, the content of parents’ annual reports, and a
cost-based fee structure.
To date there are no new published court verdicts on SG homeschooling beyond the 2022
*Verwaltungsgericht* rulings and the subsequent administrative decisions,
but SG parents remain ready to litigate denials.
The framework may yet change : the *Volksschulgesetz* is undergoing a total revision, with broad
consultation on the draft expected in 2026, and the future of private one-to-one tuition
(including the question of abolishing it) is among the contested points. The 2025 report also
expects further applications, though no longer at the pace of recent years.

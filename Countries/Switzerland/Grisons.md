# Unschooling in Grisons
## Compulsory education
In Graubünden, compulsory schooling currently lasts 11 years
(2 years of Kindergarten + Primarstufe + Sekundarstufe I),
following the revision of the *Volksschulgesetz* (VSG; BR 421.000)
adopted on 5 December 2024 and in force since 1 August 2025.
## Unschooling
The *Volksschulverordnung* (VSV; BR 421.010), in force since 1 August 2025,
governs private instruction. The provisions below are quoted from the
pre-August 2025 text (Stand 1 August 2022); article numbering and wording
should be re-verified against the currently applicable VSV.
> 2.3. Private schools and private tuition
>
> Art. 12 — Teaching authorisation
> Teachers at private schools and for private lessons must meet the same
> teaching authorisation requirements as teachers at public elementary schools.
>
> Art. 13 — Measures in the event of insufficient prerequisites
> If the private lessons or lessons at private schools do not meet the legal
> and curriculum requirements or the conditions, the department can order the
> transfer to the public school.
> The government can order the closure of private schools if they do not meet
> the legal requirements or imposed conditions.

Because private tuition must comply with teacher qualification requirements
and legal/curricular standards, fully curriculum-free or interest-driven
unschooling is not generally available in Graubünden. Authorities may require
transfer into the public school system if those conditions are not met.

Foreigners and expat families in Graubünden face the same rules as Swiss citizens.

# Unschooling in Appenzell Innerrhoden

## Compulsory education

The governing statute is the **_Schulgesetz_ (SchG)** of 25 April 2004, GS 411.000.

Per Art. 19 al. 1 SchG, compulsory schooling lasts **ten years** and comprises one year of *Kindergarten*, six years of *Primarschule*, and three years of *Sekundarschule*, *Realschule* or *Gymnasium*. It ends at the latest at the close of the school year in which the pupil reaches age 16.
 
A second year of *Kindergarten* is available as of right (Art. 18 al. 1 SchG) but is not counted towards the ten-year compulsory period (Art. 19 al. 4 SchG).


## Home education
 
### Statutory framework (SchG)
 
Two provisions in the *Schulgesetz* matter, and they pull in opposite directions.
 
**Art. 13 al. 1 SchG** states that attending a private school or receiving private instruction at *Volksschule* level is *free*.
 
**Art. 13 al. 3 SchG**, however, requires that the *provision* of private schools and the *delivery* of private instruction at *Volksschule* level be **authorised by the *Landesschulkommission*** and remain under its supervision :
 
> Die Führung von privaten Schulen sowie die Erteilung von Privatunterricht auf der Volksschulstufe bedürfen der Bewilligung durch die Landesschulkommission und unterstehen deren Aufsicht.
 
Art. 32 SchG sets the qualification standard for teachers in public schools : holders of a **cantonal teaching authorisation** (*Lehrbewilligung*), normally granted only to those holding a recognised teaching diploma (*Lehrerpatent*) for the relevant school stage and meeting the personal and professional standards of the teaching profession.
 
### Implementing rules : *Schulverordnung* (SchV) and *Landesschulkommissionsbeschluss* (LSKB SchG)
 
The credential bridge from public-school teachers to private instructors is laid by **Art. 9 al. 2 SchV** (GS 411.010) :
 
> Wer Privatunterricht anstelle der öffentlichen Schulen erteilt, muss die Anstellungsvoraussetzungen von Art. 32 SchG erfüllen.
 
The detailed operational rules sit in the ***Landesschulkommissionsbeschluss zum Schulgesetz*** (LSKB SchG, GS 411.012), Section A.IV (Arts. 22g–22k).
These contain the operative conditions for authorised private instruction in AI.
 
**Authorisation regime (Art. 22g LSKB SchG).** Both the running of a private school and the delivery of private instruction require *Bewilligung* from the *Landesschulkommission*. The authorisation is granted **for one year** and is renewable. A private-instruction *Bewilligung* permits teaching only the children of a **single family** — co-ops of unrelated children are not contemplated.
 
**Substantive conditions (Art. 22h LSKB SchG).** Six conditions must be met cumulatively :
 
1. The instructor must hold a **cantonal teaching authorisation** (*Lehrbewilligung*).
2. The **cantonal curriculum is binding**.
3. The **prescribed teaching materials must be used**.
4. The weekly number of lessons must match that of the corresponding stage in the public schools, distributed across five days, within an 8-hour daily window between 07:30 and 17:30.
5. The number of school years is binding ; the public-school promotion rules apply by analogy.
6. Particular attention must be given to social-competence education.

**Inspection (Art. 22i LSKB SchG).** The *Volksschulamt* exercises supervision. It has free right of entry to the teaching premises during the hours fixed in Art. 22h al. 4 ; can require the keeping of a teaching journal and prescribe its contents and review periodicity ; can sit examinations at locations of its choosing to verify curriculum compliance ; and specifically checks fulfilment of the educational mission of Art. 2 SchG. The *Volksschulamt* reports annually to the *Landesschulkommission*.
 
**Withdrawal (Art. 22k LSKB SchG).** The *Bewilligung* is withdrawn if the conditions cease to be met, or if the private school or private instruction fails to fulfil the educational mission of Art. 2 al. 3 SchG (which mandates education towards democracy, freedom and social justice within the framework of the *Rechtsstaat*).

## Statistics

Robust headcounts for AI are not regularly published. The most commonly cited data point comes from a 2018 *Tagblatt* survey of the four eastern Swiss cantons, which reported **zero** children in home instruction in Appenzell Innerrhoden (against 34 children in 22 families in AR, and ten in Thurgau).

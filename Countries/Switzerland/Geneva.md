# Unschooling in Geneva

## Compulsory education

Compulsory schooling in Geneva spans 11 years (HarmoS years 1–11), starting at age 4 (4 years old by 31 July, art. 55 LIP).

## Current status of unschooling

The general principle is set out in the *Loi sur l’instruction publique* (LIP, C 1 10), art. 37 al. 1 :
> Tous les enfants et jeunes en âge de scolarité obligatoire et habitant le canton de Genève
> doivent recevoir, dans les écoles publiques ou privées, ou à domicile, une instruction
> conforme aux prescriptions de la présente loi et au programme général établi par le
> département conformément à l’accord HarmoS et à la convention scolaire romande.

Home education is governed in detail by the *règlement relatif à l’enseignement privé* (REPriv, C 1 10.83), adopted on 10 May 2023 (in force 17 May 2023) and since amended (consolidated to 1 January 2025), Title III (art. 23–40). It is an authorisation regime, not a declarative one :
- Parents must request authorisation, in principle by 30 April for the following school year (art. 30) ; mid-year entry is not allowed except for new arrivals in the canton or special-education cases (art. 27 al. 2).
- The programme must conform to the *plan d’études romand* (PER) and cover all disciplinary domains, transversal capacities and general education (art. 26 al. 1).
- Exclusively-distance teaching is prohibited (art. 23 al. 2), and the child must have regular collective socialisation activities outside the family (art. 26 al. 3).
- The teaching parent must hold a title giving access to a bachelor programme at a Swiss higher-education institution (art. 28) — the *maturité gymnasiale* is the usual one, but it is not the only qualifying title.
- Authorisation is granted in principle for one school year (art. 32 al. 1) and may be made conditional or withdrawn if a condition is no longer met (art. 32 al. 2).
- Pupils sit year-end evaluations (exams or research papers) organised by the DIP (art. 34, 37) ; intermediate evaluations may be required at any time in case of doubt (art. 34 al. 4).

Where the main language of instruction is not French, the programme must include enough French lessons for the child to integrate locally (art. 26 al. 2). The regime applies equally to Swiss and foreign residents.

## Trend

The 2023 REPriv reaffirmed and detailed home education as an explicit authorisation regime (authorisation predated 2023). Subsequent jurisprudence has upheld substantial pedagogical conditions imposed under it.

In ATA/1055/2024 of 3 September 2024, the parents of a home-schooled teenager (school phobia ; largely English-language online schooling) challenged the conditions imposed by the DIP : a compulsory English exam, mandatory French lessons and a French exam, and a science research paper. They sought acceptance of the plan « sans exigence supplémentaire ». The Administrative Court rejected the appeal in full, holding the conditions lawful and proportionate — including French instruction for a French/English bilingual child and an English exam for an anglophone pupil. The sole concession (a waiver of German) had been granted by the DIP, not the court ; costs of CHF 400 were charged to the parents.

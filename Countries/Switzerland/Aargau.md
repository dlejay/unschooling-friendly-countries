# Unschooling in Aargau

## Compulsory education

In the canton of Aargau, compulsory education covers 11 years (starting at age
4, with 2 years of Kindergarten + 6 years Primary + 3 years Lower Secondary).

## Private instruction / homeschooling

According to the official website of the canton of Aargau, the educational goals and the curriculum must correspond to those of the public schools, and the costs of private schooling fall entirely on the parents :
> Private schooling
> Compulsory schooling can also be fulfilled as part of private schooling.
> The private schooling of school-age children by parents, foster parents or a third party must be reported to the responsible school leadership.
> The holders of parental authority have to ensure that their children receive sufficient education.
>
> The private schooling is under state supervision.
> The start and end of a private course must be reported to the school leadership at least 14 days in advance,
> based on the ordinance on the elementary school. As a rule, private schooling can only be started at the beginning of the semester.
> The school leadership asks the parents to provide evidence of sufficient instruction
> (regular, structured instruction, adherence to the curriculum, proof of achievement by the children, etc.).
> The school leadership is obliged to report to the Department of Education, Culture and Sport (BKS).
> Adequate teaching is checked regularly by the school supervisory board in consultation with the school leadership on site.
The canton’s stated rationale is that private instruction should not disadvantage the child when transferring to a public school, vocational training, or further education ; sufficient instruction means achieving the same educational goals under the public-school curriculum.
Swiss and cantonal schooling rules apply equally to all residents.

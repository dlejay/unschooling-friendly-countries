# Home education in Basel‑Landschaft

## Legal basis

- **Bildungsgesetz (SGS 640), § 19 Abs. 1–2** — private schools and private home schooling during compulsory schooling require a permit from the Bildungs‑, Kultur‑ und Sportdirektion (BKSD). The permit is granted if the requirements placed on public schools are met.
- **Verordnung über die Privatschulen und die private Schulung (SGS 640.43)** — sets out the substantive and procedural requirements.

## Compulsory education

Compulsory education in Basel‑Landschaft lasts **11 years** as a rule, following the Swiss HarmoS framework.
It begins with the first Kindergarten year (at age 4, cut‑off date 31 July) and ends with the Volksschulabschluss.
The two Kindergarten years are compulsory, followed by six years of Primarschule and three years of Sekundarschule.
The duration may vary in individual cases by advancement or repetition.
Home schooling must be authorised throughout this entire period.

## Requirements

**Application.** Parents must submit a *Gesuch* to the Amt für Volksschulen (AVS) of the BKSD.
The application must include a mission statement (*Leitbild*),
an organisational concept, a pedagogical concept, and details of quality assurance and development (§ 3).
The pedagogical concept must cover approach, objectives, levels, subjects,
timetable, teaching time and progress assessment (§ 9 Abs. 1).

**Deadline.** End of October of year *N* for a start in August of year *N+1* — roughly ten months lead time.

**Duration.** The permit must be renewed annually (*Wiederbewilligungsantrag*).

**Qualified teacher.** Parents or the teacher they engage must hold a subject and pedagogical qualification for the relevant school level, with a diploma recognised by the Schweizerische Erziehungsdirektorenkonferenz (EDK) or equivalent (§ 6 Abs. 3).

**Equivalence with public school.** The parents guarantee that pupils are developed — in attainment, personality, and physical and mental development — in a manner comparable to public‑school education (§ 4 Abs. 2).
They must ensure a Volksschulabschluss and the ability to transition into further education or back into the public school (§ 9 Abs. 2).

**Ideological clause.** Parents must guarantee that pupils are not exposed to pedagogical or ideological influences that fundamentally run counter to the Christian, humanistic and democratic tradition (§ 4 Abs. 2).

**Premises and infrastructure.** Adequate rooms must be available,
and access to the infrastructure needed for specialist teaching (sport, IT, *Gestalten*) must be ensured (§ 7).

**Ongoing oversight.** Private home schooling is subject to supervision by the BKSD throughout the period of compulsory schooling (Bildungsgesetz § 19 Abs. 3).

## Legislative activity

- **Interpellation 2022/369** (Laura Grazioli, 16 June 2022) — "Homeschooling : Reduktion der Hürden für die private Schulung". Answered by the Regierungsrat on 20 September 2022. The government declined to lower the requirements, arguing that private schooling must meet the same quality standard as public schooling to safeguard pupils' chances and to ensure smooth reintegration into the public system.
- **Postulat 2023/333** (Laura Grazioli) — "Homeschooling : Reduktion der Hürden für die private Beschulung", tabled for the Landrat session of 28 September 2023. (Status in the Geschäftsdatenbank to be verified if needed.)

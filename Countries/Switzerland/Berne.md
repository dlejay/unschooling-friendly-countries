# Unschooling in Bern

## Compulsory education

Education is compulsory for 11 years in Canton Bern.
This mandatory schooling period, known as the *obligatorische Volksschule*, normally starts with kindergarten after the child has turned four by 31 July, and typically runs until around age 15, subject to permitted deferred entry into the first kindergarten year.
It comprises three cycles : two years of kindergarten (Cycle 1, years 1–2), six years of primary school (Cycle 1 continued through Cycle 2, years 3–8), and three years of lower secondary school (Cycle 3, years 9–11).

## Home education

According to the *loi du 19 mars 1992 sur l'école obligatoire* (LEO ; RSB 432.210) :

> **Art. 71.**
>
> Les parents qui instruisent eux-mêmes leurs enfants ou qui leur font donner une instruction privée doivent obtenir une autorisation du service compétent de la Direction de l'instruction publique et de la culture.
>
> **Art. 71a.** Conditions d'autorisation
>
> 1 L'autorisation est octroyée si les parents garantissent
>
> a) que la mission définie à l'article 2 ou à l'article 2a est accomplie ;
>
> b) que les personnes qui dispensent l'enseignement sont guidées dans leur travail par des personnes dotées des qualifications pédagogiques requises ;
>
> c) qu'ils disposent d'équipements suffisants ;
>
> d) que les contenus et les objectifs d'enseignement assignés aux classes d'école enfantine, aux classes primaires ou aux classes générales publiques dans les niveaux d'enseignement correspondants sont respectivement transmis et atteints ; et
>
> e) que la langue d'enseignement est déterminée, sous réserve de l'alinéa 2, en fonction de la langue officielle de la région concernée.
>
> 2 Les parents peuvent être autorisés à dispenser l'enseignement dans une autre langue s'ils garantissent que les personnes qui enseignent disposent des qualifications requises.

The regime is residence-based rather than citizenship-based : children resident in Bern are subject to the cantonal compulsory-schooling framework, and newcomers may apply for an authorization to provide private instruction once the child's place of residence is in the canton. The instruction must follow the applicable Bernese curriculum for the child's linguistic region — in the French-speaking part, the *Plan d'études romand* ; in the German-speaking part, the corresponding German-language Bernese curriculum / *Lehrplan 21* framework — and be delivered in the official language of the region.

Authorization is granted by the regional School Inspectorate and the procedure typically takes 3 to 6 months ; during that time the child must remain enrolled in a public or private school. The application file must include a full annual teaching plan with reference to the relevant curriculum, a *grille horaire*, a trimester or semester calendar, and an inventory of teaching materials. A written annual report is due to the School Inspectorate by 30 June each year, documenting the curriculum points covered, the objectives reached and the teaching materials used.

## News

Bern tightened, or at least formalized, its private-instruction controls through the March 2023 guidance : applications must include annual planning tied to the curriculum, a *grille horaire*, trimester or semester planning, and teaching-material information ; annual reporting is due by 30 June. Bern's recorded homeschooling numbers rose sharply to 1,263 children in 2022/23, up from 934 the previous year. More recent EDK material describes Swiss homeschooling numbers as having stabilized after the pandemic-era increase, with around 80 % of homeschooled children concentrated in four cantons including Bern, but no later Bern-specific figure has been verified here.

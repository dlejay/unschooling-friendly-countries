# Home education in Appenzell Ausserrhoden

## Compulsory education

The governing instruments are the Gesetz über die Volksschule (Volksschulgesetz, VSG), bGS 412.00, in force since 1 August 2023, together with its implementing ordinance, the Verordnung über die Volksschule (Volksschulverordnung, VSV), bGS 412.01.

Compulsory education runs for eleven school years and begins at age four. Switzerland recognises a duty to educate, not specifically a duty to attend a public school. In Appenzell Ausserrhoden the duty may be discharged through the public Volksschule, through an authorised Privatschule, or through _Privatunterricht_ — the latter two by permit of the Amt für Volksschule und Sport (Art. 54 VSG).

## Home education

### Who may teach

Since the revised law took effect on 1 August 2023, instruction must be given or accompanied by a person holding an EDK-recognised teaching diploma for a Volksschule level (Art. 56 para. 2 lit. b VSG).
Parents without such a diploma may rely on a qualified accompanying teacher.

Under the current guideline (v1.1, December 2024), the person actually delivering the instruction must additionally hold at least a Sekundarstufe II qualification.

### Scope and limits

_Privatunterricht_ covers a single learner or a group of up to five. Above that threshold, a Privatschule permit is required instead. A _Privatunterricht_ permit is not granted where the learner requires _verstärkte Massnahmen_ — enhanced support measures for special educational needs (Art. 42 para. 3 VSV).

### Procedure, fees, duration

Applications must be submitted to the Amt für Volksschule und Sport by the end of December for a start on 1 August of the following year. During the permit process, ordinary school attendance continues to apply.
Permits are generally issued for two school years at a time.

The first permit costs CHF 300 per child. Renewal applications are free.

### Supervision

The Amt für Volksschule und Sport conducts at least one on-site visit per year (Art. 44 VSV). It may inspect teaching materials, review the learner's work, and order specific learning checks or performance assessments (_spezifische Lernkontrollen bzw. Leistungsmessungen_). The law further requires that the social integration of homeschooled children be guaranteed (Art. 56 para. 2 lit. c VSG).

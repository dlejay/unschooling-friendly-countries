# Unschooling in Basel City

## Compulsory education

§ 55 of the Schulgesetz provides that all children and young people residing in the canton are subject to compulsory schooling (*Schulpflicht*).
Compulsory schooling lasts until successful completion of the Volksschule, or at the latest until the end of the school year in which the child turns 16.

## Homeschooling

Under § 135(1) of the Schulgesetz, private instruction (*Privatunterricht*) to fulfil compulsory schooling requires authorisation from the Volksschulleitung.
Under § 135(2), authorisation may only be granted where :

(a) there are demonstrable special reasons that school attendance is not possible ;
(b) the private instruction is compatible with the child's welfare ;
(c) adequate quality of instruction is guaranteed ;
(d) the instruction is designed so that connection to the next educational level is secured ;
(e) if the child receives private instruction for more than one year, the teacher must hold a recognised teaching diploma by the second year at the latest.

Authorisation is granted for a maximum of one school year (§ 135(3)) and may be renewed (§ 135(4)) or revoked if conditions are not met (§ 135(5)).

The Federal Court confirmed in BGE 146 I 20 (2019) that there is no federal constitutional right to home-based private instruction, and that Basel-Stadt's restrictive regime does not violate Art. 13 or Art. 19 BV.

## News

A small number of families in Basel-Stadt have attempted to homeschool without permission, leading to enforcement actions by the canton. Data released by the Basel-Stadt Education Department show that in the 2022/23 school year, 11 cases resulted in fines ("Ordnungsbussen") against parents for violating school attendance law.

Media reports from prior years highlighted one Basel family that openly homeschooled two children and simply paid about CHF 4,000 in fines every year (CHF 1,000 per child, per parent) as the cost of defying the law.

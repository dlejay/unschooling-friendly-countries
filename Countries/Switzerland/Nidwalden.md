# Unschooling in Nidwalden

## Compulsory schooling

The Nidwalden _Volksschulgesetz_ (VSG ; NG 312.1) provides at Art. 33(2) that compulsory schooling begins in the second year of kindergarten and lasts 10 years, at most until completion of the _Orientierungsschule_. The first kindergarten year is voluntary ; the second is compulsory.

## Home education

Private tuition (_Privatunterricht_ / homeschooling) is governed through the private-school authorisation provisions in Art. 73 ff. VSG, which the Bildungsdirektion applies substantively to private tuition. It is subject to cantonal authorisation and supervision : the application is submitted to the Amt für Volksschulen und Sport, and the Bildungsdirektion decides after review. The Richtlinien define _Privatunterricht_ as individual instruction or instruction of no more than four pupils, unless they are from the same family.

Under the 2024 _Privatunterricht. Richtlinien_, authorisation may be granted only in justified cases and under specific circumstances. The stated condition is that the parents live with the child or children in several places worldwide and are only very irregularly present at their residence in Nidwalden, so that fulfilling compulsory schooling at the public school is unreasonable for the children or for the public school.
The application must be submitted at least six months in advance, in German, with the required documents. It is reviewed by the authority and includes an on-site inspection.

The conditions are curriculum- and teacher-based. Instruction must be given by trained teachers with the corresponding level qualification ; English and French instruction requires appropriate certificates under the European language portfolio. The Nidwalden timetable applies, and the contents of Lehrplan 21 must be implemented in the prescribed subjects. The application must show how the subjects and contents will be implemented.

Homeschooled pupils must participate in binding national and cantonal standardised assessments, including _Check Dein Wissen_, _Stellwerk_ and the checks of basic competences, with results reported to the school supervision authority. After authorisation, the responsible person must submit at least half-yearly reports on development and any challenges. The authorisation can be withdrawn during the school year if orders are not followed or the conditions are no longer met.

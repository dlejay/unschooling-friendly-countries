# Unschooling in Lucerne

## Compulsory education

In the Canton of Lucerne, attendance at the Volksschule is compulsory for all children and young people.

The Volksschule normally lasts 10 school years: 1 year of kindergarten, 6 years of primary school, and 3 years of secondary school.

## Home education

Home education / private instruction is possible only with cantonal approval.

According to the cantonal information page on approvals for private schools and private instruction, anyone wishing to provide private instruction (homeschooling) must obtain approval from the Department of Education and Culture. Applications are reviewed by the School Supervision Department of the Elementary Education Service.

According to the leaflet *Privatunterricht während der obligatorischen Schulzeit*:

> Für eine Bewilligung zur Erteilung von Privatunterricht muss die Person über eine stufen- und fachgemässe Ausbildung (Lehrdiplom) verfügen.

The leaflet also states that, in private instruction, the educational objectives of the Volksschule are binding, meaning that the curriculum of the Canton of Lucerne must be followed.

There are no special exemptions for non-Swiss or expat parents: anyone wishing to homeschool in Lucerne must meet the same cantonal requirements.

## News

Since the 2023/24 school year, any person providing private instruction during compulsory education in Lucerne must have a level- and subject-appropriate teaching diploma. The rule has applied since 1 August 2023 and appears to have contributed to the subsequent fall in homeschooling numbers.

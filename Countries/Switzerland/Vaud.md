# Unschooling in Vaud

## Compulsory education
Mandatory schooling (_école obligatoire_) in Vaud begins at age 4 and lasts 11 years.

## Home education
In Vaud, homeschoolers must follow the official curriculum (_Plan d'étude roman_).
The _Loi sur l'enseignement privé_ (LEPr, 1984) states :
> Art. 9 Home education
>
> 1. Every person who undertakes to teach at home shall notify the municipality at the beginning of each school year of the list
>    of his or her pupils.
> 2. This list shall be sent to the department, which shall check, if necessary by means of examinations,
>    that the requirements of the official curricula have been met.
Under current law (LOI du 7 juin 2011 sur l'enseignement obligatoire, LEO), parents only need to notify their local school in writing that they will homeschool.
The school then informs the cantonal education department (DGEO), and a pedagogical officer visits the child at least once a year to verify progress
(including via compulsory Canton-wide tests in French, German and math).

This declarative regime remains in force as of May 2026, pending entry into force of the revised LEPr adopted in March 2026 (see _News_ below).

## Recent developments

**Adopted (24 March 2026).** After a multi-year process, the Grand Conseil approved the revised _Loi sur l'enseignement privé_ (projet 20_LEG_73) in third debate on 24 March 2026 by 131 votes in favour and one abstention. The law replaces the 1984-vintage declarative regime with a permit system for homeschooling, and introduces an authorisation requirement to operate a private school. The Conseil d'État will fix the entry-into-force date by arrêté ; subject to the referendum deadline, the new regime is expected to take effect for an upcoming school year.

Background : the reform process began in 2018–2019 and was put out to consultation in July 2021 ; following a tragic Montreux case in early 2023 and the Education Director's announcement of a renewed draft, the Conseil d'État published its projet de loi (20_LEG_73) in July 2024 and sent it to parliament. First debate took place on 10 March 2026, second debate on 17 March 2026 (with a third debate requested), and final adoption on 24 March 2026.

Key changes once in force :
- **Authorisation Required**: Parents must obtain formal approval from the DGEO before withdrawing a child from public school or choosing to homeschool from school-start. Authorisations are issued at semester start ; emergency approvals may be granted mid-year for special cases (e.g. moving into Vaud, long-term travel, serious illness, mal-être). Renewal is by tacit roll-over from year to year as long as the situation does not change.
- **Qualifications for Tutors** : The person providing most instruction must live in Switzerland and (per the implementing règlement) hold at least a Swiss Secondary II diploma (CFC, maturité, diplôme de culture générale or equivalent).
  The transitional regime allows grandfathering : a parent who has been providing more than half of the instruction to their child for at least three years at the date of entry into force is deemed to satisfy this qualification requirement until the end of that child's compulsory schooling.
- **Curriculum Oversight** : Parents must submit a detailed teaching program in advance, aligned with the official _Plan d'études romand_ (PER) objectives. Instruction must include French (at least as a second language) and provide socialisation activities. Adaptations for special needs are allowed but must be validated by the authorities.
- **Abolition of Old Rules** : The existing ordinance on homeschooling (rLEO Art. 40) is repealed, folding all rules into the new LEPr. The 6-child threshold for triggering the private-school regime is preserved, and homeschooling already declared before entry into force will continue to be governed by the old law.

The authorisation regime brings Vaud into line with Valais, Fribourg, Jura, Neuchâtel and Geneva, where similar systems already exist. Additional staff are also being allocated to the DGEO for supervision and control of homeschooling.

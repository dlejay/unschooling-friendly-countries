# Unschooling in Uganda

## Compulsory education

Under section 11(3) of the Education (Pre-Primary, Primary and Post-Primary)
Act, Cap. 247 (s. 10(3) in the original 2008 numbering), primary education is
universal and compulsory for pupils aged six or more and lasts seven years.
All children of school-going age must enter and complete the primary cycle.

## Unschooling

According to section 6(2) of the Act (s. 5(2) in the original 2008
numbering),

> The responsibilities of the parents and guardians shall include—
>
> (a) registering their children of school going age at school;

The Act does recognise education outside conventional schools — it provides
for non-formal education programmes (s. 6(5)) and for “alternative
approaches” to basic education for children who leave school before
completing the primary cycle (s. 11(3)(c)) — but it contains no provision
allowing parents to satisfy the compulsory-primary-education requirement by
educating their children at home.

Homeschooling has never been expressly prohibited by statute, and no reported
judicial decision addressing its legality has been identified ; its
illegality rests on official interpretation. On 20 November 2024, in response
to a question from MP Molly Kamukama concerning the absence of homeschooling
provisions in the National Teachers Bill, 2024, Attorney General Kiryowa
Kiwanuka stated before Parliament’s Education Committee that homeschooling is
“technically illegal because it is not provided for under the law”. The
Permanent Secretary of the Ministry of Education, Kedrace Turyagyenda,
adopted the same position, observing that homeschooling would not form part
of the Ministry’s plans until formally introduced into the legal framework.

## Statistics

| Period          | Homeschooled children | Source                                             |
| --------------- | --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| 2024            | ≈ 15,000              | Ministry of Education estimate (*Daily Monitor*)   |
| Covid-19 period | ≈ 7,000               | Ministry/NCDC estimate reported by *Daily Monitor* |

The same figure of approximately 7,000 is attributed by Parliament Watch and
Charmar to the needs survey conducted by the National Curriculum Development
Centre (NCDC) with the Home-scholars Uganda Group (HUG), whose findings were
released in August 2024, suggesting that the sources may be recycling a
single estimate of uncertain vintage.

## News

Notwithstanding the Attorney General’s pronouncement, the government has taken
preliminary steps towards recognition. The NCDC, in partnership with HUG,
conducted a needs survey on homeschooling, and in July 2024 the *Daily
Monitor* reported that a policy blueprint under consideration would permit
parents to choose between school enrolment and instruction at home. According
to the Ministry spokesman, Dr. Denis Mugimba, the contemplated framework would
comprise a standardised NCDC curriculum binding on parents, inspections
conducted by district school inspectors, assessments by experts designated by
the Ministry as a condition of progression between levels, and prescriptions
on daily study time, with the South African model serving as a benchmark.

On 20 March 2025, the NCDC formally presented its study report, *A Needs
Survey on Homeschooling in Uganda*, which recommends that the government
recognise and regulate homeschooling. As of July 2026, however, no such
policy has been adopted, and the practice remains outside the legal
framework.

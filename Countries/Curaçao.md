# Unschooling in Curaçao

## Compulsory schooling

Curaçao's compulsory-schooling regime is governed by the
_Leerplichtlandsverordening_ and related implementing rules. The duty is not
framed as a general right to home education, but as a duty on parents/
guardians to enrol the child in a school and ensure regular attendance.

The leerplicht runs from the school year beginning on or after the child
turns 4 until the end of the school year in which the young person turns 18,
unless an eligible diploma is obtained earlier.

The official _Leerplicht informatie bulletin_ summarises the duty as follows :

> A. Enroll his/her child(ren) in a school;
>
> B. Ensure that the child attends all daily classes.

## Statutory exemption test

Under Art. 4(2) of the _Leerplichtlandsverordening_, exemption may be granted
where the parents/guardians have overriding objections to the _direction_
(_richting_) of all schools in Curaçao that the child could attend, **and**
they show that the child receives sufficient education in another way.

The Gemeenschappelijk Hof van Justitie has held, by analogy with the settled
Hoge Raad case-law on the equivalent Dutch Leerplichtwet 1969, that
_richting_ refers to a fundamental orientation grounded in a specific
religious conviction or _levensbeschouwing_. Pedagogical or didactical
objections — i.e. objections to the _inrichting_ (organisation) of education
rather than its direction — fall outside the scope of Art. 4(2) and do not
ground an exemption (ECLI:NL:OGHACMB:2021:206).

## Ministerial application of the test

Recent reporting states that the ministry applies this as a narrow exemption
route : parents must demonstrate significant objections to the direction of
all existing school organisations on the island, not merely objections to the
organisation or quality of schooling. According to Curaçao Chronicle :

> To qualify for an exemption, parents must demonstrate significant objections to the direction of all existing school organizations on the island. The evaluation showed that in recent years, parents have not sufficiently met this criterion. Most objections were related to the organization of education, not to its ideological direction.

> The number of exemption requests has increased over the past five years. This has led to stricter policies starting in the upcoming school year. Parents who receive a rejection will be required to comply and enroll their child in a school.

## Recent developments (2024–2026)

- The Homeschooling Association Curaçao (HAC) has publicly contested the
  policy tightening, arguing that new criteria (pedagogical qualification,
  certificate of good conduct) were applied to the 2024/2025 cohort without
  prior notice and without consultation, despite an earlier ministerial
  invitation for HAC to submit a policy proposal.
- The Algemene Rekenkamer (Oct 2024) and the Ombudsman (Feb 2026) have
  flagged structural failures in leerplicht enforcement — relevant as
  background to the ministry's stated rationale for stricter exemption review.

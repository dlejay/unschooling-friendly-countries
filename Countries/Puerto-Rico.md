# Unschooling in Puerto Rico

## Compulsory education

School attendance is compulsory for children aged 5 through 18 (3 L.P.R.A. § 143b).

## Unschooling

The Constitution of the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico states :

> Section 5. Every person has the right to an education which shall be directed to the full development of the human personality
> and to the strengthening of respect for human rights and fundamental freedoms.
> There shall be a system of free and wholly non-sectarian public education.
> Instruction in the elementary and secondary schools shall be free and shall be compulsory in the elementary schools to the extent permitted
> by the facilities of the state.
> No public property or public funds shall be used for the support of schools or educational institutions other than those of the state.
> Nothing contained in this provision shall prevent the state from furnishing to any child non-educational services established by law
> for the protection or welfare of children.
>
> Compulsory attendance at elementary public schools to the extent permitted by the facilities of the state as herein provided
> shall not be construed as applicable to those who receive elementary education in schools established under non-governmental auspices.

Puerto Rico law expressly recognises homeschooling as a lawful educational option. Article 2 of Act 33-2017
(18 L.P.R.A. § 2322) recognises the choice of a licensed or unlicensed educational option, including
"homeschooling", as a fundamental parental right.

Homeschooling is treated as education under non-governmental auspices rather than as part of the
public-school system. No approval, registration, or notification to any government agency is required,
and no periodic testing or formal reporting is mandated.

No prescribed curriculum, government approval of the educational programme, or mandated assessment
applies. Parents therefore retain broad freedom over curriculum and teaching method, subject to the
general obligation to provide an adequate education.

For children under 16, HSLDA interprets 29 L.P.R.A. § 450 as requiring the same number of instructional
days as the public schools. This concerns instructional time rather than curriculum or teaching method.

The DEPR's _Carta Circular núm. 10-2020-2021_ governs the convalidation of homeschool studies when a
student seeks admission or readmission to the public-school system ; it does not establish ongoing
supervision of an operating homeschool.

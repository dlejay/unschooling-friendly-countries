# Unschooling in Albania

## Compulsory education

Full-time basic education (_arsimi bazë_) is compulsory for children from age 6 to age 16. It comprises nine grades (grades 1–9) : five years of primary education and four years of lower secondary education. Upper secondary education is not compulsory.

## Unschooling

In Albania, home education is available only in special cases and is not a general parental alternative to school attendance.

The Law on Pre-university Education (Law No. 69/2012, as amended) states :

> Article 17
>
> Education in home conditions
>
> Home education is offered in special cases, for all classes of basic education or only for some of them.
> The Minister defines the special cases, criteria and procedures for education in home conditions.

Article 17 does not itself create a general right to parent-led home education, and the current implementing framework does not establish a procedure through which parents may choose homeschooling as an alternative to school attendance.

The home education expressly provided for in the current framework is a school-system service for pupils who cannot attend school. Article 64 (6) of the law requires the local education unit, under criteria and procedures established jointly by the ministers responsible for education and health, to provide home education to pupils who cannot attend an ordinary or specialised basic-education school. Article 81 of the Regulation on the functioning of pre-university educational institutions — approved by Order No. 31 of 28 January 2020, as subsequently amended by Order No. 300 of 19 June 2024 and Order No. 420 of 27 August 2025 — likewise requires the local education office (ZVAP) to provide education at home to pupils with disabilities who cannot attend an ordinary or specialised basic school (per the text as consolidated with the 2024 amendments).

The previous 2013 Normative Dispositions were repealed by Order No. 31 of 28 January 2020.

There are no special homeschooling rules for expatriates.

# Unschooling in Belize

## Compulsory education

Compulsory education in Belize covers children aged 5 to 16 inclusive. Under section 59 of the Education and Training Act, 2010, the duty of attendance applies to a child “who is five years old by the first day of September” and within compulsory school age. The upper limit was raised from 14 to 16 by the Education and Training (Amendment) Act, 2024 (No. 3 of 2024, assented 21 February 2024, gazetted 24 February 2024), which amended the definition of “child of compulsory school age” in section 2 and replaced “fourteen” with “sixteen” in section 59.

## Unschooling

Section 59 of the Education and Training Act, 2010 requires every person having control of a child of compulsory school age to cause the child to regularly attend a public or nonpublic school. Where the Chief Education Officer considers that a parent is failing in this duty, section 60(1) provides for a notice requiring the parent to satisfy him that the child “is receiving suitable education either by regular attendance at school or otherwise”.

Rule 10(6) of the Education Rules provides :

> Home schooling of children of compulsory school age may be allowed where the
> parents satisfy the Chief Education Officer that there is good reason to so do
> and that the parents possess the competence and the capacity or will make the
> necessary provision for the proper education of the child.

The Ministry issued Home Schooling Protocols in August 2020. According to the Belize Home Schooling Protocols :

> A. The parent/legal guardian requesting authorization for home schooling must :
>
> 1. reside in Belize ; proof of residence must be ascertained
> 2. demonstrate academic competence equivalent to at least an Associate degree for
>    the designated home schooling teacher (i. e. the parent/legal guardian or other
>    identified practitioner) ; authenticated credentials must be submitted.

Also, in Addendum B : home schooling agreement,

> 2. The Parent/Legal Guardian undertakes to take personal and direct responsability
>    for the education of the child to provide at home which is in accordance with
>    the appropriate curricular learning outcomes as outlied by the Ministry ;

The Protocols make residence in Belize, not citizenship, the stated eligibility criterion.

## News

In February 2024, the National Assembly raised the compulsory education age from 14 to 16.

# Unschooling in the Netherlands

## Compulsory schooling

The Leerplichtwet of 1969, states all children must attend school.

> **Artikel 2. Verantwoordelijke personen**
>
> **1** Anyone who has legal authority over a minor, as well as anyone who is actually responsible for the care of the minor, is required under the provisions of this law to ensure that the minor is enrolled as a pupil at a school and regularly attends that school after enrollment. Upon enrollment, an official government-issued document must be submitted, indicating the minor’s surname, initials, date of birth, gender, and citizen service number (BSN). If a BSN is not available, the student’s education number must be submitted if possible. If the persons referred to in the first sentence demonstrate at the time of enrollment that they are unable to provide either the BSN or the education number of the minor, they must submit it to the school as soon as they obtain it.

Children must start schooling at age 5 and continue until 16, after which there is a partial compulsory education requirement (_kwalificatieplicht_) requiring teenagers under 18 to keep studying or follow part-time vocational training.

## Compulsory schooling exemption

The main legal route used by homeschoolers is getting a schooling exemption under Article 5(b) for directional/religious-philosophical objections to nearby schools.
This is an exemption from compulsory school attendance, not a general legal right to homeschool.

## Recent developments

**Public Prosecutor stopped enforcing (April 2025).** The Public Prosecution Service (OM) stopped prosecuting compulsory education cases involving Art. 5b exemptions since April 2025, citing inconsistent municipal criteria and unpredictable legal outcomes.
This is a major de facto loosening — but not a legal reform.


**Government exploring two radical options (Dec 2025).** State Secretary Becking's December 2025 letter to Parliament outlines two directions: either make homeschooling a recognized way to fulfill the compulsory education duty (open to all parents, not just those with religious objections), or scrap the Art. 5b exemption entirely, forcing all children into schools. Parliament to be updated before summer 2026.

**Quality-assurance pilot running 2025–2026.** A pilot is being conducted in 2025 and 2026 testing different forms of quality assurance for homeschooling, involving organizations like the Stichting Keurmerk Thuisonderwijs and the NVvTO.
Participation is voluntary.

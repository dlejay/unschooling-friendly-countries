# Unschooling in the British Virgin Islands

## Compulsory education

Under the Education Act, 2004 (as amended in 2014), compulsory school age runs from 5 to 17 years (s. 2, s. 28(1)). Every child must attend school unless excused, notably where the Minister, after consultation with the Chief Education Officer, is satisfied that the child "is receiving satisfactory instruction at home or elsewhere" (s. 39(1)(a)).

## Unschooling

Home education is governed by sections 89–90. The parent must register the student with the Chief Education Officer before the programme starts and annually thereafter (s. 89(2)). According to section 89(3), as amended in 2014 :

> (3) An educational plan for each student who is receiving home education shall be prepared and provided to the Chief Education Officer subject to the following conditions:
>
> (a) the initial educational plan shall be prepared and provided to the Chief Education Officer three months prior to the commencement of the home education programme;
>
> (b) the educational plan shall be for a minimum period of three school years and shall cover every year of the home education programme;
>
> (c) the educational plan shall include a description of the learning activities for the student that will comply with the goals and objectives set out in section 4 (2); and
>
> (d) the educational plan shall be based on the national curriculum as established by the Minister under Part 8, or any appropriate curriculum from an approved institution.

The Chief Education Officer must provide for the regular assessment of the student’s achievement (s. 89(7)) and *shall* terminate the programme if the student fails to meet standards of achievement, as measured by achievement testing, comparable to those of public-school students (s. 90(1)(b)), with an appeal lying to the Education Appeal Tribunal.

## News

In January 2023, the Education Minister stated that the review of the Education Act was in its final stages of completion. The 2026 Speech from the Throne subsequently announced the Government’s intention to revise the Act in areas including education governance and accountability, child protection, curriculum standards covering S.T.E.A.M., digital literacy and national heritage, and professional standards for educators. As of July 2026, no legislation changing the home-education provisions has been enacted ; the Education Act, 2004, as amended in 2014, remains in force.

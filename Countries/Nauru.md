# Unschooling in Nauru

## Compulsory education

Under the Education Act 2011 (No. 6 of 2011), as amended, each parent of a school-age child must ensure the child is **enrolled at a school** until the child completes the school year during which the child turns 18 (s. 8(1)). A "school-age child" is a child who, on 1 January of the year, is at least 4 and under 18 years of age (s. 4) ; the school year runs from 1 January to 31 December, so a child remains covered throughout the calendar year in which they turn 18.

This enrolment obligation does not apply to the parent of a child registered for home education, or of a child holding a certificate of exemption (s. 8(2)). Attendance obligations (s. 14) only apply to children enrolled at a school.

Fixed-penalty enforcement was introduced in 2025 : the Education (Amendment) Act 2025 inserted the statutory notice procedure (ss. 113A–113F), and the Education (Fixed Penalty Notices) Regulations 2025, which commenced on 13 September 2025, designated offences under s. 8 and certain other provisions as fixed-penalty offences. The fixed penalty for contravening s. 8 is $25 for a first offence and $125 for a subsequent offence.

## Unschooling

A parent of a school-age child may apply to the Secretary for registration of the child for home education (s. 97) ; if only one of two parents applies, the other is notified and may make submissions. Registration is discretionary :

> **98 Grant of registration for home education**
>
> (1) The Secretary may grant the registration of the child for home education for a period of not longer than 2 years if satisfied that:
>
> (a) the home education will not be harmful to the interests of the child; and
>
> (b) the conditions of registration will be complied with.

Registration is subject to conditions (s. 99) : the responsible parent must provide "high-quality education", document the educational opportunities offered to the child and the strategies used to encourage the child to learn, and make education programmes, materials and records available for inspection on request. An annual progress report to the Secretary is required (s. 103). Registration is renewable (s. 102) and may be cancelled for breach of conditions after notice (s. 100) ; refusals and cancellations are reviewable by the Minister (ss. 105–107).

The Act does not require registered home education to follow the approved school curriculum, prescribe particular subjects or mandate a specific teaching method. It therefore does not preclude education organised around the child's interests, provided the responsible parent can demonstrate that it constitutes high-quality education, document the educational opportunities and learning strategies used, and report annually on the child's progress.

The Act does not restrict home-education registration by citizenship ; the scheme applies to school-age children in Nauru.

# Unschooling in Japan

## Compulsory schooling

According to the 学校教育法 (School Education Act):
 
> **Article 17§1** — A guardian shall be obligated to have his/her child attend elementary school, the first semester of a compulsory education school, or the elementary department of a special-needs school from the beginning of the first school year on and after the day following the day on which the child reaches the age of six years until the end of the school year that includes the day on which the child reaches the age of twelve years.
>
> **Article 17§2** — Custodians have the obligation to enrol their child in a junior high school, the upper course of compulsory education school, the lower course of secondary education school, or a junior high school course of a special needs education school from the first school year that begins on or after the day following the day on which the child has completed the programme of an elementary school, until the end of the school year that includes the day on which the child reaches 15 years of age.

### MEXT futōkō attendance-recognition mechanism (2019)
 
MEXT issued guidelines allowing school principals to count home-based ICT learning as attendance days for enrolled-but-non-attending (futōkō) students.
This requires meeting 7 conditions, including: close parent-school relationship, IT-based learning activities, face-to-face guidance, a structured learning plan, and principal oversight.
 
This is not a homeschooling framework.
It is an accommodation for school refusal within the existing enrolment system.
The child remains enrolled ; the principal retains discretion.
It does not create a right to homeschool.

## Foreigners / expats
 
Compulsory schooling does not apply to non-Japanese nationals.
 
Art. 26§2 of the Constitution uses 国民 (*kokumin*, “the people/nationals”).
MEXT interprets this as excluding non-citizens.
Foreign guardians have no legal obligation to enrol their children in school.
 
A 2019 MEXT survey found ~ 20,000 foreign compulsory-age children (nearly 1 in 5) were not receiving any school education — highlighting that absence of obligation also means absence of institutional support.

## Futōkō statistics (MEXT)
 
| Academic year | Non-attending elementary + lower-secondary students | % of total |
|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 353,970 | 3.86 % |
| 2023 | ~340,000 | ~ 3.7 % |
 
This is the 12th consecutive annual increase.
The 2024 survey also recorded 769,000 bullying cases and 413 student suicides — all record highs.

# Unschooling in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines
## Compulsory education
Section 2 of the Education Act defines compulsory school age as follows :
> “compulsory school age” means from five years of age to sixteen years of age;

i.e. 11 years of compulsory education, starting at the age of 5 years. A UNICEF fact sheet
from July 2021, referencing 2018 data, is consistent with this.
## Context
Section 21 of the Education Act gives every parent of a child of compulsory school age the choice of
home education “in accordance with section 111”. This is not a notice-only regime :
section 15(2)(d) provides that the Chief Education Officer may “permit the student to undertake a
home education programme”, and section 40(2)(h) excuses a child from compulsory school attendance
only where the child is “receiving home education … in accordance with this Act”. Under
section 40(3) and 40(4)(a), a parent relying on home education must apply to the Chief Education
Officer for a certificate of exemption from attendance, which the Chief Education Officer “may
grant or refuse to grant”.

According to section 111 of the Education Act :
> (2) The parent shall, prior to the commencement of a home education programme for the child and
> every year thereafter for as long as the home education programme is provided, register the child
> with the Chief Education Officer.
>
> (3) An educational plan for a child who is receiving home education shall be prepared and provided to the Chief Education Officer
> and shall comply with the following conditions—
>
> (a) the initial plan shall be prepared and provided to the Chief Education Officer three months prior to the commencement
> of the home education programme;
>
> (b) the plan shall include a description of the learning activities for the student that will comply with the goals and objectives
> set out in section 3(3);
>
> (c) the plan must be based on the national curriculum as established by the Minister under Part VIII; and
>
> (d) the plan shall be for a maximum period of three academic years with details for each year;
> and may be extended subject to the approval of the Chief Education Officer.

This law applies to children of compulsory school age residing in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines (section 15(1)).
## News
In September 2024, NBC Radio reported that the Ministry of Education, through School Attendance Officer Kenroy Boucher,
was reminding parents and guardians of the necessity to follow established guidelines when transitioning children from traditional schooling to home education.
Notably, the report states that the specific requirements parents need to follow would be provided to them after permission to homeschool is granted by the Chief Education Officer.

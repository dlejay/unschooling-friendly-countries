# Unschooling in Poland

## Compulsory education

Three successive obligations apply : one compulsory year of preschool preparation
from age 6 (obowiązek rocznego przygotowania przedszkolnego) ; compulsory schooling
(obowiązek szkolny) from the beginning of the school year in the calendar year the
child turns 7, until completion of primary school but no longer than age 18 ; and a
compulsory-education obligation (obowiązek nauki) until age 18. All three may be
fulfilled outside an institution under Article 37.

## Context

According to the Law on School Education (among other things) :

> Article 37. 1. At the request of the parents, the head of the public or non-public kindergarten,
> elementary school and secondary school, respectively, to which the child has been admitted, may permit, by decision, for the child to fulfill,
> respectively, the obligation referred to in Article 31, paragraph 4, outside the kindergarten,
> kindergarten division in an elementary school or other form of kindergarten education and the obligation of schooling
> or compulsory education outside the school.
>
> […]
>
> (4) A student fulfilling compulsory schooling or compulsory education outside of school shall obtain annual classification grades
> on the basis of annual classification examinations of a part of the core curriculum applicable at a given educational stage,
> agreed upon for a given school year with the school principal. Classification examinations are conducted by the school,
> the principal of which has authorized the fulfillment of compulsory schooling or compulsory education outside the school.
> Such a student shall not be assessed for behavior. Classification examination shall be conducted in accordance with Article 44l
> or Article 44wa of the Law on the Education System and regulations issued pursuant to Article 44zb
> of the Law on the Education System.

The application may be filed at any time, including mid-year ; the director decides
by administrative decision (within 30 days under the Code of Administrative
Procedure), with appeal to the kurator oświaty in case of refusal. Required
attachments : a parents' declaration that conditions enabling realisation of the
core curriculum are ensured, and a commitment to annual classification examinations.
The former requirements of a psychological-pedagogical counselling-centre opinion
and of enrolment within the voivodeship of residence were repealed in 2021. No
classification examinations are held in art, music, technology, physical education
or religion/ethics, and no behaviour grade is given. Since the examinations cover
the national core curriculum, learning cannot be purely interest-driven.

Foreigners are subject to the same rules as nationals.

## Recent developments

Since 1 January 2026, the education-subsidy calculation for schools enrolling
home-educated pupils is sharply reduced beyond 96 such pupils per school
(previously 200) : the first 96 are weighted at 0.8, while pupils above 96 are
weighted at 0.2 where home-educated pupils are under 30% of enrolment, and 0.4
where they are at least 30%. Parents' rights under Article 37 are untouched, but
some umbrella schools have introduced fees as a consequence.

The admissibility of online classification examinations remains contested.
Article 37 itself does not spell out the examination format, but ministry-side
interpretations rely on the assessment regulations : classification exams are
written and oral and, as a rule, conducted at the enrolling school. Schools
serving home educators — notably Szkoła w Chmurze, which alone enrols
c. 26,000 pupils in 2025/26 — dispute this reading.

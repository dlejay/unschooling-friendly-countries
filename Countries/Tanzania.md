# Unschooling in Tanzania

## Compulsory schooling

Statutory compulsory schooling is governed by section 35 of the Education Act,
Cap. 353. Section 35(1), as amended by Act No. 10 of 1995, removed the original
upper age limit ; the provision now requires enrolment from the age of seven,
and obliges parents to ensure regular attendance until the child completes
primary education :
> **35.**-(1) It shall be compulsory for a child who has reached the age of
> seven years to be enrolled for primary education.
>
> (2) [The parent of every child enrolled for primary education shall ensure
> that the child regularly attends the primary school at which he is enrolled
> until he completes primary education.]
The provision is framed generally, applying to “every child”, without limiting
the duty by reference to citizenship. A subsequent policy reform — the 2014
Education and Training Policy, 2023 Edition — provides for extending the
compulsory period to ten years (six years of primary and four of lower
secondary education), but this extension has not been enacted into statute.

## News
The 2014 Education and Training Policy (2023 Edition), implemented from January
2024, introduces a 1+6+4+2/3+3 structure under which primary and lower secondary
education — ten years — would be compulsory and free. Implementation is phased,
with completion targeted for 2027.
In December 2025, the Ministry of Education identified substantial gaps in the
National Education Act, 1978, including the duration of compulsory education. On
21 May 2026, the government initiated a formal review of education legislation ;
the Education Act Review Committee (Cap. 353) submitted its preliminary report,
and a draft bill is to be published for public consultation. As of 22 May 2026,
section 35 of the 1978 Act remains the operative statutory requirement.
Zanzibar legislates separately on education and mandates basic education for
children of primary-school age.
In 2024, the government announced prosecutions against parents concealing
disabled children from school.

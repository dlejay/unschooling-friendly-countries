# Unschooling in Chile

## Compulsory education

Compulsory education in Chile comprises basic education (_educación básica_) and
secondary education (_educación media_); the Constitution also makes the second
transition level of kindergarten (_Nivel de Transición 2_) compulsory, although
early-childhood education is not a prerequisite for entry into basic education.

Secondary education became constitutionally compulsory through Ley N° 19.876 of
2003, which also required State financing of a free system up to age 21.

## Home education

Article 19 No. 10 of the Constitution recognises the right to education and the
parents’ preferential right and duty to educate their children; Article 19
No. 11 protects freedom of education, including parents’ right to choose the
educational establishment for their children.

The General Education Law (_Ley General de Educación_, Ley N° 20.370 of 2009),
Article 41 provides that a Supreme Decree of the Ministry of Education regulates
the validation of learning acquired outside the formal system and the
convalidation of studies equivalent to basic or secondary education completed
abroad. The procedures are set by Decreto Exento N° 2272 of 2007 (amended by
Decreto Exento N° 708 of 18 July 2024).

For studies completed abroad, the route is convalidation where applicable. For
studies undertaken from within Chile outside the recognised Chilean school
system, the relevant route is validation through _exámenes libres_, not
convalidation.

Per Mineduc guidance for the 2026 process, under-18 validation exams are
available to minors who have not undertaken regular studies, have interrupted
them, have studied in institutions not officially recognised by the State, are
foreign residents in Chile, are returning Chileans without access to
convalidation, or are special-education pupils seeking to complete secondary
education. Minors must be registered by a parent or legal guardian; private
institutions such as homeschools or _escuelas libres_ cannot register them.

A minor who was enrolled during the same school year in an officially recognised
educational establishment generally cannot register for validation exams that
year, except in justified cases such as health reasons or other cases expressly
accepted by the Ministry.

## Statistics

| Year | Under-18 validation-exam registrations |
| ---- | -------------------------------------- |
| 2023 | 33,331                                 |
| 2013 | 7,592                                  |

These figures are a proxy for alternative education and homeschooling, not a
direct count of homeschoolers. Using formal enrolment of 3,631,025 students in
2023 as the denominator, the 33,331 figure equals about 0.9 %; this should not
be read as a precise homeschool share.

## News

In late May 2025, the Supreme Court, in Rol N° 16.519-2025, confirmed the
Santiago Court of Appeals’ rejection (Rol N° 18.799-2024) of an action
challenging Mineduc’s refusal to convalidate secondary studies completed from
Chile through the United States-based “Home Life” academy. The studies were
carried out at home in Chile under an online programme offered by a US
institution, so the requirement that studies be completed abroad was not met.
The alternative route indicated was validation by _exámenes libres_.

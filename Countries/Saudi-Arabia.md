# Unschooling in Saudi Arabia

## Compulsory education

Public education is compulsory for citizens and resident children in Saudi Arabia.

Compulsory education covers the elementary and intermediate stages: nine school years, normally from approximately age 6 to age 15. Elementary school lasts six years and intermediate school three years.

## Home education

Saudi law does not provide a general right for parents to withdraw a child of compulsory-school age and educate the child independently at home.

The Executive Regulations of the Child Protection Law require a child’s father, guardian or authorised representative to enrol the child in school. They must not disrupt the child’s education. Schools must monitor attendance, investigate absences and report cases where necessary. The regulations also require authorities to prevent children from dropping out prematurely and to encourage regular attendance.

Saudi Arabia has traditionally offered an **affiliation** or external-study route (_intisāb_), under which a student studies outside ordinary daily classes but remains attached to a school and sits prescribed examinations. This is not autonomous homeschooling or unschooling: the student remains within the official education system and must satisfy its admission, curriculum and examination requirements.

In March 2025, the Ministry of Education directed schools to stop admitting students who are still within the normal school-age range through the affiliation route and to return existing school-age affiliate students to regular attendance. Where a student has a medical condition supported by medical reports, the case may instead be referred to the Ministry for an exceptional decision.

Affiliation remains available principally to students who are older than the normal age for regular schooling and wish to continue or complete their education.

Consequently, free-form, parent-directed unschooling is not a recognised alternative to school attendance for an ordinarily school-aged child in Saudi Arabia.

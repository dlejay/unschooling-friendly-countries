# Unschooling in Uzbekistan

## Compulsory education

Uzbek law provides for a 12-year compulsory education structure :

- 1 year of mandatory preparation for school (ages 6–7) ;
- 11 years of general secondary school (Grades 1–11, ages ~7–18 ; admission to Grade 1 in the year the child turns 7).

The 11 + 1 structure (eleven years of schooling preceded by a compulsory one-year preparation programme for children aged 6–7) is established in the 2020 Education Law (no. ZRU-637, art. 4). Article 50 of the 2023 Constitution makes general secondary education compulsory and free and places preschool education and upbringing under state supervision. A presidential decree of February 2026 further provides for the one-year school-preparation programme to constitute the initial stage of compulsory general secondary education, with electronic completion certificates to be issued from the 2027/2028 school year.

## Legal framework for family education

Article 18 of the 2020 Education Law (as amended through December 2025) recognises “education in the family” :

> **Article 18. Education in the family and self-education**
>
> The state provides assistance in obtaining education in the family and self-education. The education of children in the family and self-education are carried out according to the relevant curricula with the provision of methodological and advisory assistance.
> The procedure for obtaining education in the family and self-education, as well as the categories of students are determined by the relevant authorized state bodies in the field of education.
> Education in the family is carried out on the basis of an agreement between a state educational institution and parents or other legal representatives of students, taking into account the interests of children, families, the state and society.
> Self-education is carried out on an individual basis and contributes to the professional, intellectual, spiritual and cultural development of students.
> The issuance of a state standard education document to persons who have received education in the family and through self-education is carried out in accordance with the approved curriculum of state educational institutions in the order of external study.

## Implementation

No publicly identifiable implementing procedure appears to have been adopted for the general population : the “procedure” and the “categories of students” that Article 18 leaves to the authorised state bodies remain, as far as can be established, undefined. In addition :

- the only home-education procedure administered by the Ministry of Preschool and School Education concerns children with physical or psychological development issues or in need of long-term treatment, granted on the basis of a medical commission’s conclusion (ministerial regulation of 10 June 2015, reg. no. 2691) ;
- family education would in any event require an agreement with a state educational institution and adherence to state curricula, precluding an interest-driven approach ;
- school principals bear legal responsibility for enrolling every child of school age residing in their catchment area, with district hokims monitoring full coverage.

## Eksternat

The only certification route open to self-educated persons is *eksternat* (external assessment) : those who have independently mastered the general secondary curriculum within the state educational standards may sit the state final assessments, free of charge, at designated schools, from age 16 for the Grade 9 (basic secondary) certificate and from age 18 for the Grade 11 certificate. Eksternat thus provides retrospective certification of self-education ; it does not constitute a legal basis for a child of compulsory school age to remain outside school.
